    The Fall of the House of Usher: The importance of New Year's Eve party and celebrations explained

    23 Aug 2024, 00:00

    The New Year's Eve party and celebrations in the episode "A Midnight Dreary" of The Fall of the House of Usher play a significant role in the overall narrative, offering insight into the characters, their relationships, and setting the stage for future events. This article will delve into the importance of the New Year's Eve party and celebrations, exploring their symbolism and impact on the storyline.Firstly, the New Year's Eve party serves as a backdrop for various revelations and discussions among the characters. As partygoers count down to the New Year while "Another Brick in the Wall" plays in the background, the atmosphere is festive and energetic. This celebration provides a stark contrast to the dark and mysterious elements of the Usher family's past, creating a sense of tension that permeates the entire episode. The juxtaposition of the vibrant party atmosphere with the underlying secrets and guilt harbored by the Usher family intensifies the intrigue and anticipation for what is to come.The preacher's speech during the party also adds depth to the significance of New Year's Eve. The preacher's words about conquering the fever of "living" and the presence of spirits of the dead create an eerie and ominous atmosphere. This speech foreshadows the dark events that will unfold in the Usher family's life and hints at their deep connection to the supernatural.Furthermore, the New Year's Eve party serves as a catalyst for conversations that shed light on the characters' past, motivations, and relationships. During the party, Roderick Usher, the protagonist, interacts with reporters and receives a mysterious phone call from someone wanting to meet him. This encounter leads Roderick to a conversation with a person who offers expensive cognac and shares condolences for the losses Roderick has experienced.Through this conversation, the audience gains insight into Roderick's upbringing, specifically his strict and abusive mother, Eliza, and her role as a secretary for the CEO of Fortunato Pharmaceuticals. Roderick recalls an altercation with the CEO, Mr. Longfellow, and how his mother refused medical help, relying on faith instead. This revelation deepens the audience's understanding of Roderick's complex family history and the profound guilt he carries.The New Year's Eve party also sparks discussions among the Usher family members about past events and the potential informant within their ranks. Roderick and his sister Madeline speculate about the informant's identity, suspecting Camille, a young relative, and expressing concerns about their stepmother, Juno. These discussions reveal the distrust and suspicion within the family, adding layers of intrigue and mystery to the narrative.Moreover, the New Year's Eve party serves as a thematic device that highlights the passage of time and the impending resolution of conflicts. As partygoers countdown to the New Year, the audience is reminded of the continuous progression of time and the imminent arrival of the courtroom trial against Fortunato Pharmaceuticals and the Usher crime family. The celebration symbolizes the turning of a new year, offering hope for resolution, justice, and perhaps redemption for the Usher family.In conclusion, the New Year's Eve party and celebrations in "A Midnight Dreary" play a crucial role in the narrative of The Fall of the House of Usher. The party serves as a backdrop for revelations, discussions, and tensions among the characters, providing insight into their pasts, motivations, and relationships. Furthermore, the party symbolizes the passage of time and the impending resolution of conflicts, heightening anticipation for the courtroom trial and the events to come. Through its various layers of symbolism and narrative impact, the New Year's Eve party serves as a pivotal moment in the story's progression.
