    The Hayden Orpheum Cinema found themselves in a little bit of a pickle after posting a cheeky photo following 'Student Night' at a showing of Fifty Shades Darker.

    The retro cinema in Mosman, north shore of Sydney, posted a photo of a cucumber, which had been found after the screening of the romance movie Fifty Shades Darker - the sequel to Fifty Shades of Grey.

    While it could be a clever and dirty PR stunt, nevertheless the Orpheum cinema team posted the photo on social media to watch it go wild.

    The cucumber posted to the Oprheum's social media account after Fifty Shades Darker

    The Hayden Orpheum found the humour in the  discovery 

    The Fifty Shades Darker movie is the sequel to Fifty Shades of Grey

    Social media users were quick to point out whoever is posted holding the cucumber might have to vehemently give their hands a wash.

    When the Orpheum was inundated with comments on Twitter and Facebook asking why a staff worker was handling the evidence they said the 'lights were down' making the cucumber unrecognisable.

    One user commented: 'And you pick it up without gloves?'

    The Oprhemum responded with: 'instantly regretted that!'
