    Miss American 2014 Winner - Pageant Beauty Tricks

    Media Platforms Design Team We've seen our fair share of new beauty trends emerge on the Spring 2015 runways, but sometimes it's fun to get inspiration from a totally different kind of backstage...perhaps even as far off the fashion map as Atlantic City.

    12 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Media Platforms Design Team

    We've seen our fair share of new beauty trends emerge on the Spring 2015 runways, but sometimes it's fun to get inspiration from a totally different kind of backstage...perhaps even as far off the fashion map as Atlantic City.

    BAZAAR went to the Miss America competition this weekend, and while the pageant has in recent years taken great measure to distance itself from the stereotypical beauty pageant image (did you know Miss America is the largest provider of scholarships to young women in the world?), there's still a thing or two to learn from the gorgeous contestants. Here, the five wildest tips I learned from the ladies backstage:

    1) Procure butt glue. We know it may sound strange, but it's perhaps the oldest—and most infamous—trick in any pageant girl's book. The contestants often use it to keep their swimsuits in place during the lifestyle and fitness portion of the show, but let's face it: it would keep any wardrobe malfunctions, from Montauk to Mumbai, at bay, and if you're too ashamed to buy it, some contestants use hairspray or even WD-40. The trick is to find one that's waterproof to keep away sweat in the heat.

    2) Buy a lemon. No, these aren't used in post-competition cocktails. Several contestants use lemon peels the day of competition to whiten their teeth. While it may not seem the most logical, one can at least admit it's cost-effective. When life gives you lemons, flash those pearly whites.

    3) Pack a nail file. For manicures and pedicures and…teeth. Yes, it's been said that in a bind, a clean nail file will do the trick to take that snaggle into a dazzle if you're feeling pressed for time. It may not be safest, but it does the trick if your smile needs the straight and narrow.

    4) Don't wash your hair. Ever. While hair extensions and even wigs are no stranger to the pageant stage, some contestants—whether out of superstition or savviness—don't wash their hair for up to two weeks prior to the big night. Major volume is maintained and the natural oils leave hair looking extra shiny. But if you're worried about excessive shine throughout the process, baby powder and dry shampoo are at the ready to do the trick.

    5) Smile always. Sometimes using petroleum jelly on your teeth also helps, but in a bind, never forget the oldest mantra in the book: you're never fully dressed without a smile.

    Despite all these tips, it takes real guts (tanned and toned, of course) to go on national television in any capacity, let alone in a swimsuit. The woman with most guts and glamour this year was Kira Kazantsev, who won the title as Miss New York (the state's third consecutive winner—a competition first.) Miss Virginia, Miss Arkansas, Miss Florida and Miss Massachusetts rounded out the elusive top fice. Kazantsev played a cup routine a la "Pitch Perfect" to Pharrell's "Happy" for her talent (watch the impressive video below) and plans to attend Notre Dame law school after her year of service. She walks away with a $50,000 scholarship—and hey, despite all the hullabaloo over whether pageants are still relevant, you have to admit there's nothing more beautiful than brains.
