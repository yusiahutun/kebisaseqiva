    An avid rugby player and sports enthusiast, Welsh presenter Gethin Jones was never likely to shy away from a challenge that required him to tone up in just six weeks.

    Now the 37-year-old has unveiled the results of taking part in a six-week swimming challenge and although he finds being considered a hunk uncomfortable, admits that 'it's nice to look this good'.

    He also revealed that he thinks men are becoming more body-obsessed than ever, but says that isn't always such a bad thing.

    Hunk: Gethin Jones shows off the ab-tastic results of a six week swimming challenge  

    'Men are becoming more obsessed with their figures than women,' he says in an exclusive interview with MailOnline.

    'Some of my friends have started coming swimming with me after they realised how my body has changed since the challenge.

    'I don't think there's anything wrong with that, There's a real difference between vanity and wanting to look your best. It's good to feel good and looking good makes you feel good.' 

    The challenge, which involved swimming at least three times a week, was part of the Get Speedo Fit campaign, which aims to highlight the benefits of swimming.

    Jones, who says he gained muscle but lost a large percentage of his body fat and boosted his aerobic fitness by 13 per cent, is clearly a convert.

    Body conscious: Jones says men are becoming as obsessed with their physiques as women 

    Before and after: Although the 37-year-old has put on weight, he has lost body fat and increased muscle

    'I put weight on, but lost body percentage fat and increased muscle,' he explains.

     'I swam as a child and always loved the water. When I first started the swimming challenge I could manage maybe six or seven lengths. 

    'But the improvements I have made since then are immeasurable. The tests show that I increased my aerobic fitness by 13 per cent, my leg and arm strength increased by just under 10 per cent and my flexibility increased by one fifth (20 per cent)

    'Just last week I did a three-kilometre swim - so going from six or seven lengths to this is amazing.' 

    Jones is no stranger to physical challenges, having been a semi-finalist on Strictly in 2007 and completed an epic 1,000-mile sailing, running and cycling challenge for Sport's Relief in 2012.

    Nevertheless, he says he is surprised that the pool gave him back his six-pack so quickly, even though regular swimming felt considerably less painful than going to the gym.

    'I’m 37 now and the body can ache after a session of running or at the gym, but with swimming I didn't get that at all and I also don’t have to warm up before.

    'I can't remember the last time my body was this toned,' he adds. 'I was surprised by the before and after pictures.'

    Big improvement: Jones says he can't remember the last time his body was this toned 

    Tests: As well as being more muscular, tests showed Jones' flexibility had increased by 20 per cent 

    The shots, which show him leaping out of the pool, abs rippling and with a suitably sexy smoulder plastered across his face, have, he admits, 'gone down okay' with the opposite sex.

    'I had plenty of help with choosing the after photos for the campaign but I couldn't work out if people were trying to be kind or not,' he says bashfully.

    'Of course it's nice to look this good. Every man has a bit of vanity - I just hope I don't have too much.' 

    Ageing, although making the gym more of an endurance test than it used to be, has wrought improvements in his mindset and, he adds, left him more confident about his body than ever before. 

    'I'm less body conscious than I used to be,' says the 37-year-old. 'I've come to realise that you get the body your parents give you. 

    Looking good: Despite his rippling new abs, Jones says he still feels uncomfortable with female attention

    Helping out: The bespoke fitness programme was tailored by Speedo instructor Dan Bullock (pictured right)

    Pleased: Jones says he enjoys looking 'this good' and has continued to swim since completing the challenge

    'I'm quite skinny in my structure, like my father. My sister always jokes that she wishes that she had my legs.' 

    Jones currently splits his time between Los Angeles and London and says he has learned to love the outdoorsy Californian lifestyle.

    Still single since splitting from Katherine Jenkins in 2011, Jones says he is focusing on work, among them a stint hosting a Dancing With The Stars.

    'It was amazing, but weird being back in a ballroom scenario,' he beams. 'The set looks the same, and feels the same - the only difference is there's no Bruce Forsyth, Tess Daly and Claudia Winkleman.' 

    The show also featured a starrier line-up than the UK's Strictly Come Dancing - a factor that led to an encounter with Bruce Willis and Demi Moore for Jones.  

    'Well, you know, the pictures have gone down okay': The presenter has been modest about the 'after' images

    Focused: Jones presented the most recent series of Dancing With The Stars which was won by Rumer Willis

    'Rumer Willis ended up winning and that was a surreal moment,' he says. 'I remember being sat next to Rumer in the make-up trailer and Demi Moore was there giving feedback.

    He adds: 'On the first day I parked in Bruce Willis' car parking space thinking that would really funny. Obviously that wasn't.

    'It was quite nerve-wracking meeting him,. You expect him to be in a white vest top with blood on him. What do you call him? Bruce? Mr W?

    'I think I just mumbled "Mr Willis, how do you do?" When in doubt, go super British and polite, it never fails.'
