    A BBC BREAKFAST presenter was left red-faced as a festival-goer gatecrashed a live interview with a rude comment.

    As usual Charlie Stayt and Naga Munchetty presented this morning's show from Manchester.

    The pair brought viewers the latest news from around the country.

    During the show Charlie, 60, and Naga, 48, crossed live to Donington Park where this year's Download Festival takes place.

    Hannah Miller was standing by to present the segment as the show covered the event, which kicked off on Thursday.

    But due to one festival-goer Hannah's report had to end and the journalist handed back to the studio shortly after an incident.

    It led Hannah to apologise to viewers at home before Charlie and Naga returned to screens.

    Meanwhile, Naga has received an outpouring of support from BBC Breakfast fans as she posted an inspiring photo after sharing a health battle.
