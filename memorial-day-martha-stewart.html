    Just Back From: Skylands. Martha Stewart's home in Seal Harbor, Maine.

    Trip Duration: The long holiday weekend.

    Travel Buddies: This year, our crew included Photographer Douglas Friedman, Editorial Crafts Director Hannah Milman and Stylist and Creative Director Kate Berry. We didn't even make it to Martha's before stopping at Down East Lobster Pound for the freshest lobster rolls, the best coleslaw and the sweetest corn.

    Where You Stayed: Skylands, Martha's summer home in Maine. I have been working with Martha for 21 years, and have been summering in Maine with her for almost just as longâ€“she bought Skylands 18 years ago and we've celebrated Memorial Day there ever since.

    The Weekend Begins...with a summer kick-off game of croquet. This year, the #campskylands crew was invited by Martha's favorite neighbor to participate in a friendly match. But, it's hard to focus on the game when you have a mallet in one hand, a cocktail in the other and a sunset to die for.

    Hannah Milman @hannahcmilman

    Best Things I Ate: Obviously, when you're dining at Martha's house, each and every thing is perfectly prepared, homegrown and handmade. The weekend started off with Martha breaking down a 50lb locally-caught halibut, which we enjoyed all weekend. She made use of the entire thing; steaks, salt broiled collar, chowder, burgers, hash, fish and chips and fish stock to last the rest of the summer. On our second day at Skylands, we prepared the most delicious lunch of Cuban sandwiches, packed with quality ingredients: David Chang Slow Roasted Pork Loin, Schaller Weber Swedish Ham, Jarlesberg Cheese, Martha's homemade sweet pickles, Balthazar Bakery's Pain du Siegle bread, mayonnaise, Grey Poupon Dijon Mustard, Vermont Creamery slow turned butter, and jarred fire-roasted red peppers. These supreme ingredientsâ€“plus a hard-core panini makerâ€“equal seriously delicious sandwiches. Confession: they were so good I had TWO! And, no visit to Maine would be complete without a lobster dinner.

    Douglas Friedman @thefacinator

    Douglas Friedman @thefacinator

    Drink of Choice:  After endless activities, cocktail hour at sunset is beyond rewarding. Not only is the environment inspiring, but the pantry is always stocked so we could whip up anything on the fly. We all have our roles in the cocktail prep: Douglas was the muscle and squeezed all the limes and grapefruits, Kate mixed the citrus with orange liqueur and tequila to make pink margaritas with "no sugar" at Martha's request and Hannah mixed pink, black, and gray salt to mimic the color of the pink granite that paves Martha's roads to Skylands. As for me, I taste test. It's A Good Thing.

    Kate Berry @kateberryberry

    Best Hang Out Spot: The top of Beehiveâ€“it's a nearly vertical summit we climb yearly. This was our first hike of the season on one of Martha's favorite trailsâ€“with a 6 a.m. wake up call. To earn your breakfast you are required to scale the mountain, but it's one of the most special ways to see the island. After the hike, we prepared an all-American breakfast of scrambled eggs, bacon and delicious breads toasted up.

    Best Daytime Activity:  For a weekend full of activities, it's nice to get away and have a quiet, zen moment at the Asticou Gardens. It was designed by Beatrix Farrand, one of the most justifiably celebrated gardeners in the U.S. in the 1900's. Fun fact: Beatrix was Edith Wharton's niece.  Another Memorial Day tradition is a visit to Surrey Gardens Nursery to pick up the finishing touches for all the pots we plant on Martha's terracesâ€“they make the most inspiring hanging baskets which is my favorite part of the nursery. I love the color combos and inventive use of plant material.  In the afternoons, we love to walk along Little Hunters Beach; it's a nice walk from Martha's where we love to take all the dogs. The rocks on this beach are round and have a special palette that has inspired us all in the work we do at the office.

    Favorite Shopping Trip:  No weekend would would be complete without a tag sale. Who knew Douglas collected porcelain dolls?! Then, we roam the farmer's markets. Although we pretty much grow and make everything we eat, one of the things we can't live without is the yogurt and dairy from Smith Family Farm. They are one of the many local family-run farms at The Bar Harbor Farmers Market, and we always come here to stock up to take back to NYC with us.

    Terre Blair @terre.blair

    Trip Highlight:  Kate and I both cannot wait to get out to Martha's cutting garden on Memorial Day weekend to harvest the peak lilacs. Martha grows more than seven different varieties. The fragrance is intoxicating, the conversation flows and memories are made while we create beautiful arrangements for the house. I love to get my quality time in with friends doing the things we love.
