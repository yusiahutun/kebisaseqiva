    Some skin care products and steps are, thankfully, pretty self-explanatory. Face wash cleanses your face, an exfoliator buffs away dead skin cells, and a moisturizer, well, moisturizes. Serums, however, aren’t quite so straightforward. Serums vary widely in terms of what purposes they serve in your skincare routine. 

    So, which serum is best for you? It depends. “I always encourage patients to think about their specific skincare needs first when choosing a serum,” says Corey L. Hartman, MD, board-certified dermatologist and founder of Skin Wellness Dermatology in Birmingham, Alabama. To help you do just that, we scoured the web for the best serums for over a dozen different complexion concerns. Find your perfect match(es) below.

    Active Ingredients for Your Skin Goals
    • Hydration: Hyaluronic Acid 
    • Dark Spots, Uneven Tone: Vitamin C 
    • Hyperpigmentaion, Irritation: Niacinamide (Vitamin B3) 
    • Fine Lines, Aging: Retinol and Retinol Alternatives 
    • Acne: AHAs and BHAs

    Best Overall

    L'Oréal Paris Pure Hyaluronic Acid Serum

    Walgreens

    What We Love: It’s a multitasking hydrating and plumping formula that works well for all skin types and tones. 

    What We Don’t Love: Some users with rosacea claim that the serum can exacerbate redness.

    Formulated with 1.5 percent hyaluronic acid, this lightweight serum sinks in to deeply hydrate and plump the skin. The fragrance-free formula is gentle enough for sensitive skin, while being effective enough to serve as a steadfast serum in mature skincare routines. In that way, it’s a universally-appealing serum for any complexion or skincare regimen.  Some rosacea sufferers had some flare-ups, though, many more claim that it’s plenty gentle. And when we tried it on our own sensitive skin, we had no qualms. 

    For best results, this serum should be applied to a damp face. Hyaluronic acid is a humectant, so it works best when it has moisture to grab onto and be drawn deeper into the skin.

    Key Ingredients: Hyaluronic acid, vitamin C | Skin Type: All | Best For: Skin in need of plumping hydration | Size: 1 oz

Truly, when using this serum, we marveled over just how hydrated our skin felt all day long. And after just a few weeks of use, we noticed that our breakouts were becoming less and less common. Suffice to say, you can’t go wrong with this HA serum.

    Best Vitamin C

    L'Oreal Paris Revitalift Vitamin C Face Serum

    Target

    What We Love: It’s affordable, effective, and the packaging makes it easy to enjoy every last drop. 

    What We Don’t Love: Immediately out of the tube, the serum’s texture is more like a paste.

    When we researched and tested the most popular vitamin C serums on the market, we found the L'Oréal Paris Revitalift 10% Pure Vitamin C Face Serum to be the best of the best. Formulated with 10 percent pure vitamin C, the serum (which is more like a paste until it melts into your face) is a potent solution for brighter-looking skin. 

    Not sure you need a brightening boost? Dr. Hartman advises it for just about everyone. “When applied topically, vitamin C reduces damage to our cells by neutralizing free radicals, which are highly reactive and harmful molecules formed by exposure to environmental stressors, such as pollution and UV light,” he says. The ingredient also inhibits melanin production, so it’ll help fade dark spots and hyperpigmentation, evening out the complexion. 

    Dr. Shamban says that the face should be completely dry, as vitamin C is notoriously unstable and could degrade if exposed to water, or even another skincare formula. “It should dry with a waiting period of two or three minutes before your layer of sunscreen or moisturizer,” she says.

    What to Keep in Mind

    • Skincare Concern: When shopping for a serum, the most important thing is to consider your goals for your skin. Your goals — not the goals everyone’s speaking to on TikTok. “Following serum trends or choosing a serum based on an influencer recommendation is not always going to work for your specific skin needs,” says Dr. Hartman. With this in mind, think about how your skin performs on a monthly basis as well as how it is overall, and select your serums from there. “Some skin concerns are temporary, based on factors like seasonality, time of the month, lifestyle, nutrition, stress, and other factors, so as our skin changes, our need for boosting does too,” says Dr. Shamban.
    • Active Ingredients: The active ingredients to look for in a serum should always align with your unique complexion concerns — except for hyaluronic acid (for hydration) and antioxidants (to prevent and repair photodamage), which everyone can and should use. As a general rule of thumb, if you have dull skin, an uneven tone, or dark spots, look for a serum with vitamin C and/or niacinamide; if you’re concerned about aging or wrinkles, retinol and retinol alternatives are worthwhile; if you struggle with acne, ingredients like glycolic acid and salicylic acid can work wonders. (FYI: Concentration matters! Dr. Hartman says to seek out concentrations of 10 to 20 percent of vitamin C, 10 to 15 percent for AHAs, and .05 to 2.5 percent for BHAs.)
    • Texture: In many cases, the texture of a serum is more of a personal preference, with no one being necessarily better than another. That said, some skin types do better with certain textures — for example, oily complexions may prefer watery formulas, gels, or gel lotions to help keep skin hydrated while preventing pore congestion, says Dr. Shamban, while dry or sensitive skin may respond better to a lipid-based serum. What matters most when considering texture is whether or not you plan on layering multiple serum formulas. “A thin, milky formula tends to have a runnier consistency than a lightweight gel, so you’d want to apply the thinner formula first,” explains Dr. Hartman. 

    Your Questioned, Answered

    Do face serums really work?

    Yes, so long as you choose effective formulas and apply them properly. “The active ingredients in serums tend to be composed of smaller molecules that can be better absorbed into the skin, going deeper into the skin for maximum efficacy,” says Dr. Hartman. Then, your moisturizing creams serve as a barrier, sealing in the nutrients from your serum and adding their own benefits. Notice how we said the moisturizer seals it in? If you apply serums over moisturizer, they won’t be able to penetrate the cream and thus won’t be effective. 

    How do you apply serums on your face?

    Serum should be applied consistently, AM and PM (unless otherwise noted), to achieve the best results. They should also always be applied to clean skin. “You want the best absorption so serums are most effective immediately after cleansing, before moisturizing and sunscreen (in the morning)," says Dr. Shamban. 

    How many serums should I use?

    Less is more, says Dr. Hartman. “Most people would be fine with using one serum in the morning and another serum at night, but if you really want to use more than one, layer two serums max on your face.” You can also rotate an alternate formula in and out of your routine if your skin needs something different on a particular day. Just don’t feel the need to stack several serums into your morning and evening routines. “​​You don’t want to add so many active ingredients to your face that you risk irritation,” warns Dr. Hartman.
