    The Copernicus Science Centre is named after Nicolaus Copernicus.

    This iconic institution pays homage to the famous Polish astronomer who proposed the heliocentric theory, revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

    It opened its doors to the public in 2010.

    The Copernicus Science Centre has been captivating audiences with its innovative exhibits and engaging programs for over a decade.

    The Centre covers an impressive area of over 20,000 square meters.

    Visitors have plenty of space to explore and immerse themselves in the fascinating world of science.
