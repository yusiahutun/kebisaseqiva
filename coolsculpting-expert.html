    ‘Body contouring’ has grown from beauty buzzword to big business, with CoolSculpting considered the safest non-surgical treatment available at clinics and medi-spas globally. Using controlled cooling (i.e. freezing), it targets pockets of stubborn fat – ones often resistant to dieting and exercising, and promises permanent results.

    As with many cosmetic procedures, it comes with a lot of questions: Is it safe? Is it always successful? Does it hurt? To answer the above, and more, we spoke to medical aesthetician Ria Murch of The Cosmetic Skin Clinic who explains the ins and outs of the cryolipolysis system.

    What areas of the body does CoolSculpting best work on?

    “It allows us to sculpt and re-contour most areas of the body now, especially with the new CoolAdvantage heads that have a bigger tissue draw and can treat even the smallest of areas. Most commonly treated areas include the abdomen, ‘muffin top’ area, back and bra fat, and even the double chin.”

    How does it work on the double chin?

    “Patients who are unhappy with their chin have no way of hiding it and heaviness in this area can make the face look more aged than its years. Narrowing this area makes you generally appear slimmer and can take years off you.”

    Who is the ideal candidate?

    “The most ideal candidates are those that eat a healthy diet and exercise. As long as they have subcutaneous fat that is ‘pinchable, grabbable fat’ they are likely to be a good candidate for this treatment. CoolSculpting can also be used in curvier patients to reduce the fat as part of a weight-loss lifestyle programme.”

    What does it feel like? Is pain relief used?

    “CoolSculpting is generally a comfortable treatment. You may feel a pulling sensation when the applicator goes on. After a few minutes of the cooling process the area will feel numb. You can then lie and relax until the applicator comes off. Usually there is no downtime at all. But with any medical treatment, there is a recovery period for your body, so you may feel a bit tender and numb for a few days to two weeks afterwards. This does not generally interfere with your daily life and you can exercise as normal. We would advise anyone after treatment to wear loose comfortable clothing and avoid pressure on the area for at least a week post-procedure. If necessary, we recommend paracetamol to manage any discomfort.”

    How long does it take?

    “The treatment takes 35-45 minutes per applicator, an average is four-six (but this is often performed in one session). When treating multiple areas such as the abdomen and waist, we can often apply two machines in order to halve the treating time. In the clinic we routinely do this; it is called DualSculpting.”

    Where does the body fat actually go?

    “The fat cells affected by the CoolSculpting procedure are metabolised naturally before being excreted via your body’s own lymphatic system.”

    Are there any risks and side effects?

    “Side effects vary between patients; possible post-treatment discomfort may be experienced and can include short term redness, swelling and/or numbness in the area treated, particularly when larger areas are treated. However, this is often an indication of positive results and these side effects are temporary and generally pass within a day or at most a week – they also do not stop you going about your daily life. The CoolSculpting procedure is the only FDA-cleared cryolipolysis fat reduction treatment available in the UK. With over five million Coolsculpting procedures worldwide, the treatment is proven to be safe and effective for non-surgical fat reduction.”

    How long does it take to see results?

    “Results can start to show in four weeks with optimum results at eight-twelve weeks. The skin quality improvements will continue for longer, so we like to review patients at eight weeks and 16 weeks to see the overall treatment-to-transformation.”

    How long will the results last?

    “The results are permanent because once the treated fat cells are frozen they crystallise and die – so once they’re gone they’re gone for good. As long as you maintain a healthy lifestyle the long-term results should remain stable.”

    Should you combine CoolSculpting with exercise and dieting?

    “There is no need to diet and exercise to make the treatment work. It doesn't replace the need to maintain a healthy lifestyle which should be an ongoing part of your life. It just eliminates the stubborn pockets of fat that exercise and dieting can’t reach which can make it even more motivating.”

    Does it matter about gain weight or becoming pregnant post-procedure?

    “If you return to your pre-pregnancy weight and maintain a healthy lifestyle you will still appreciate the results. Once the fat cells have gone they should not return to the area treated.”

    Why is CoolSculpting considered the most effective and safest fat reduction treatment?

    “The Harvard scientists behind the CoolSculpting device have devised the perfect temperature to destroy the fat cells at minus 11 degrees Celsius with an inbuilt patented safety mechanism called Freezedetect. Over five million procedures have been carried out worldwide, The Cosmetic Skin Clinic has carried out over 12,000 procedures with a very high level of patient satisfaction.”
