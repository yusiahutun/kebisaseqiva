    London Breed's family faced many challenges while she was young. She grew up in public housing with her siblings and her grandmother, Comelia Brown. Unfortunately, her younger sister died of drug abuse in 2006.

    Her brother, Napoleon Brown, is in prison, serving a 44-year sentence for a 2000 conviction on charges of murder and armed robbery.

    She attended Galileo High School, a public high school in San Francisco. She then enrolled at the University of California, graduating in 1997 with a bachelor's degree in political science. In 2012, she completed her master's degree in public administration from the University of San Francisco.

    Professional career

    London Breed's political career began with her service on the San Francisco Redevelopment Agency Commission and her appointment by then-Mayor Gavin Newsom as a supervisor for District 5. She also served as the president of the Board of Supervisors from 2015 to 2018.
