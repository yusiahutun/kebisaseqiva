    Who is Brice Rhodes?

    He is a Louisville man and ex-rapper who was arrested in 2016 for the murder of Christopher Jones, Maurice Gordon, and Larry Ordway. The bodies of Gordon, 15, and Ordway, 14, were found on May 22, 2016, near F. S. 41st Street and River Park Drive.

    Christopher Jones' death occurred on May 4, 2016, when Rhodes shot him to death by mistaking him for someone else. A co-defendant in the case said that the two teenagers were in the car with Rhodes when he killed Jones. He then stabbed the two teens and burned them.

    What followed Brice Rhodes' arrest?

    The American convict was arrested, and then a couple of investigations followed. During the time, Brice Rhodes' mother was contacted, and she said that her son was mentally unstable.

    Some medical tests were done on Brice, and it was ruled that he was competent to stand trial. However, the judge also mentioned that Brice was intellectually disabled and had suffered from a mental illness for almost all of his life.

    So, was Brice Rhodes released? Brice was not released; instead, he remained in prison, although the district judge ruled that Rhodes would face life without parole as the harshest sentence. While still in prison, Rhodes faced more charges of terroristic threatening and assault.

    On October 19, 2017, Rhodes was accused of refusing to return to his cell, becoming violent with a corrections officer, threatening to kill a female correction officer, and allegedly throwing and hitting an officer with a bottle of urine.

    During a court hearing, Rhodes threatened Amber Wolf, a Jefferson District Court Judge, saying he'd get out and find her family.

    Where is Brice Rhodes now?

    Is Brice Rhodes still alive? The ex-rapper, 32, is alive and in prison. He has been appearing in court waiting for his verdict for seven years. His next trial will begin on December 11. Brice Rhodes' verdict will follow shortly after.
