    Oh, the 90s! What was up with television shows? It was an era plagued by a common theme of shocking TV scenes that seemed to sell quite well. People were obsessed with crazy tales, and hosts would take extreme measures to pull off something phenomenal. However, in 1995, things got out of control when a guest on The Jenny Jones Show took it a notch higher by committing a murder. It is now known as the Jenny Jones murders, still famous more than two decades later. So what went down that caused the bizarre incident?

    What would you do if you were embarrassed on live TV? Cry, get angry, laugh it off? Of course, the last thing on your mind is killing the person who embarrassed you, but it happened in 1995. Jonathan Schmitz did the unthinkable by shooting his admirer.

    Appearance on the Jenny Jones Show

    The 90s was a confusing time for TV. It is hard to categorize programming during the era as it involved violence, sex, profane language and much more negatives that would seem bizarre today. And that is when The Jenny Jones Show was a hit.

    It was a plotted talk show hosted by comedian/host/singer Jenny Jones. Also, the television show was produced by Quincy Jones-David Salzman Entertainment and Telepictures Productions.

    The show ordinarily tackled unusual subjects that were bringing in high ratings. And they handled things like confronting former bullies, paternity tests, out-of-control teens, people who had crushes on other people and more.

    Jonathan Schmitz's acceptance of an invitation to appear on the show was on the premise that a lady had a crush on him. And of course, he went to the show expecting the secret crush to reveal herself. Unfortunately, however, matters didn't go as he envisioned.

    The big betrayal

    According to Lt. Bruce Naile, when Jonathan arrived at the studio, he saw a lady he knew and thought she might be his secret admirer.

    He figured she was his secret admirer and walked up and kissed her. But then they told him: 'Oh, no, she's not your secret admirer.'

    The person referred to as his secret admirer was Scott Amedure, a 32-year-old acquittance of Jonathan's. Schmitz knew Scott through a mutual friend, and he was surprised to be informed about him being his crush, which he did not expect. And since he had agreed to do the show, he was unaware of his rights, and he went along with it.

    The show's producers stated that they mentioned that his crush might be a man or a woman and was not specific about the gender. Unfortunately, the episode never made it to TV, but an excerpt came out primarily where Jonathan is informed about his gay crush.

    Jonathan Schmitz murders Amedure

    If you have watched the footage of the TV show when Jonathan is informed that his crush is a man, you wouldn't imagine that the same person would end up killing his supposed crush. But it happened a few days after taping the TV show.

    According to reports, after the TV show, Jonathan went out for a long night of drinking. He returned home in the morning and noticed a note left by Scott at his door. What later transpired is quite unimaginable.

    Jonathan got agitated by the advances, and he immediately withdrew sufficient money to buy a .12 gauge shotgun and went straight to Amedure’s home.

    First, he asked Scott if he had written the note left on his door. He affirmed that, indeed, he left the note there. Then, Jonathan returned to his car and contemplated his next move. In a short while, he grabbed his shotgun, went to Amedeur’s home and shot him twice in the chest before calling 911.

    What happened after the murder?

    After shooting Amedeur, Jonathan called 911 and confessed everything. And the ensuing trial was as famous as The Jenny Jones Show. It attracted a lot of media attention.

    During the trial, Jonathan's defence attorney argued that his client was diagnosed with manic depression and Graves’ disease. He stated that he was pushed to commit homicide because of mental illness and humiliation. The defence attorney used the gay panic defence.

    Jonathan was later convicted of second-degree murder in 1996 and was sentenced to 25 to 50 years in prison. However, in 1999, Schmitz's conviction was overturned, and he was re-convicted of the same crime.

    The Amedure family lawsuit for $25 million

    Another exciting thing about the Jerry Jones murder case is that the deceased's family sued "The Jenny Jones Show for $25 million. They blamed the television show for the death of their son in a civil suit. And it was successful at the beginning as a Michigan court awarded the family $25 million.

    However, the ruling was later overturned after an appeal stating that the show “had no duty to anticipate and prevent the act of murder committed by Schmitz three days after leaving the studio.”

    Jenny Jones murders documentary on Netflix

    The popular streaming platform Netflix has a documentary series that re-examines Jenny Jones's infamous talk show episode linked to the murder in 1995. It's a true-crime series called Trial by Media and is executive-produced by George Clooney.

    The primary interest of the series is to examine cases in which intense media interest played a role. Other episodes in the documentary series feature other cases and personalities like Bernie Goetz.

    Schmitz was granted parole in 2017

    If you are looking for the Jenny Jones murders update, you might be surprised to know that Jonathan was released from prison in 2017. He was released according to the parole he was granted following another hearing.

    Why did Jenny Jones get cancelled?

    After the murder, The Jenny Jones Show continued to air but was eventually cancelled because of poor ratings. It was no longer viable since it was a syndicated show, and it was not getting picked up by numerous markets as expected. The show’s final episode aired in May 2003, after the civil lawsuit was overturned.
