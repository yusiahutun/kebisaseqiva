    Celine celebrates the Sixties with autumn/winter 2024

    Each season, we break down everything you need to know about the new collections in The Fashion Week Cheat Sheet. After speaking to the designers about their inspiration, their hero pieces, the faces on the catwalk and the names on the front row, we present your complete guide to autumn/winter 2024.

    31 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Each season, we break down everything you need to know about the new collections in The Fashion Week Cheat Sheet. After speaking to the designers about their inspiration, their hero pieces, the faces on the catwalk and the names on the front row, we present your complete guide to autumn/winter 2024.

    Although fashion month officially wrapped in Paris last week, the Celine show has only just taken place with the house choosing to show off the official schedule again this season. The collection – which was entitled 'La Collection de l’Arc de Triomphe' – was dedicated to Slimane's friend and mentor, the photographer Richard Avedon, and was filled with references to the 1960s.

    In-keeping with its current strategy of hosting audience-free shows, but streaming digitally, this collection – which was a mix of ready-to-wear and couture – made a splash for its distinctive Sixties-inspired pieces and for a big announcement, that Celine would be launching beauty for the first time in the house's history.

    Courtesy of Celine

    Courtesy of Celine

    Theme and inspiration

    This collection was all about celebrating the golden age of Celine, the brand explained, looking back at the Sixties and recapturing the origins of the house.

    "In 1971, Celine Vipiana’s car broke down on Place de l’Etoile, whilst waiting for her car to be recovered she noticed the chain link encircling the Arc de Triomphe, a decorative pattern that became Celine’s iconic monogram," the house said in the show notes. "Upon arriving at Celine in 2018, Hedi Slimane reintroduced a new version of the house’s 'Triomphe' emblem, appearing on the Triomphe bag which he designed on his first day becoming the house’s new classic, one that embodies the very essence and values of the couture house."

    Courtesy of Celine

    The clothes directly referenced the era – there were no trousers shown in the entire collection, only dresses and skirts, while there were a number of two-piece co-ords that were typical of the Swinging Sixties. This is of course a huge departure from Slimane's usual collections, of which skinny jeans and trousers have tended to dominate.

    Knee-high boots, shift dresses, mini skirts, faux-fur jackets, statement felt caps, pinafore dresses, pearls and polo-necks also featured.

    Courtesy of Celine

    Courtesy of Celine

    Hero pieces

    This collection was quite a departure of what we've seen from Celine in recent years, and the feminine skirt suits were the best example of this. Meanwhile, the breathtaking embellishment on some of the couture pieces were real highlights and the house also presented three new bags; the Nino (a top-handled mini), the Terence (a chic shoulder bag) and Garance (a patent crossbody), all of which featured the iconic Triomphe emblem.

    Courtesy of Celine

    Courtesy of Celine

    The setting

    The collection was presented in multiple venues, all of which were chosen to celebrate historical Art Deco interiors in Paris. Much of the show was filmed in the mirrored room in the music venue, La Salle Pleyel. Other shots took place in Le Musée Bourdelle, Le Musée des Arts Décoratifs and La Maison de la Chimie.

    Courtesy of Celine

    Courtesy of Celine

    The takeaway

    With the reveal of the house's new collection, Celine also made a big announcement, that it would be heading into beauty for the first time in its near 80-year history. Celine Beauté will be the house's first cosmetics line, and is an expansion from Celine’s Haute Parfumerie collection, which launched in 2019. Of course, all the models in the autumn/winter 2024 show were wearing the make-up, specifically the 'La Peau Nue' lipstick, one of the 15 shades in the Le Rouge Celine collection. The first shade, 'Rouge Triomphe' will launch this autumn, while the rest of the line will launch in 2025.
