    At times, grooming is essential because one’s outfit speaks volumes about them. For this reason, some men invest in their looks by purchasing classy outfits, such as suits. Often, such men include celebrities, business moguls, and top political leaders. Some of them own the most expensive suits to have ever been designed in the world.

    The fashion industry is, undeniably, among the most lucrative sector. Nowadays, some designers make outfits more expensive than a luxury car. While most people cannot afford such outfits, some have the financial capability to purchase them.

    List of most expensive suits in the world

    How much is the most expensive suit? One many ask. The most expensive one can be compared to the price of a three-bedroom bungalow, new luxury car, or both. Below are the most expensive suits in the world and their approximate prices to help you understand:

    10. Ermenegildo Zegna Bespoke – $22,000

    This piece is made by Zegna, which is an Italian company. They have highly experienced tailors. The firm has been in the business for more than 100 years. It manufactures for notable brands such as Tom Ford, Gucci, and Yves Saint Laurent, among many others.

    Ermenegildo Zegna Bespoke is not only light but also durable. Usually, one piece may take more than three days to make. Approximately, a single piece can pass through 200 steps before it is complete.

    Lastly, multiple fitting sessions are involved before it is finally shipped to the customer.

    9. World Wood Record Challenge Cup Suits – $28,000

    The concept behind this design was derived from a popular competition, World Wool Record Challenge Cup. Luigi and Loro Piana organize it. The contestants are, precisely, allowed the opportunity to make the best suits for men.

    The winner hands over her/his product to the brothers, who then later weave it and cut it into 50 pieces before selling them on their behalf. In 2019, Susanne Triplett, based in New South Wales, won the competition. The most expensive suit 2020 winner is yet to be announced.

    8. Brioni Vanquish II – $43,000

    Brioni Vanquish II is made from the ultra-luxurious Vanquish II fabric. It contains the most expensive and rare materials to find in the market. Usually, the designer, Brioni, makes a hundred pieces only a year. Brion’s popular clients are Donald Trump and James Bond.

    7. Desmond Merrion Supreme Bespoke – $47,500

    Desmond Merrion is a renowned British tailor based in London. He is behind this expensive make, which is currently going at $47,500. It falls in the category of the most expensive tailored suits.

    Unlike some designers, Merrion guarantees his customers legit hand-sewed garments such as this one. He also takes enough time to assess the quality of his crafted work before delivery. Perhaps, this alone justifies its price tag.

    6. Kiton K-50 – $60,000

    Kiton is a renowned brand in Italy and the rest of the world. The firm has been in existence for more than six decades now. Usually, each piece takes more than two days to stitch. Also, it must pass through the hands of 45 tailors before it is handed to the customer.

    K-50 is a name derived from the time it takes the designer, Enzo D'Orsi, to stitch one suit, 50 hours. It can also denote the number of pieces he makes per year, which is 50 pieces.

    5. William Westmancott Ultimate Bespoke – $75,000

    William Westmancott Ultimate Bespoke is one of the nicest suits in the world. It is made by William Westmacott, who is a Savile Row craftsman. The tailor has made the garment for prominent people such as Sultan of Oman and the late Michael Jackson.

    Usually, one garment takes experts up to 200 hours to design. The end result is an ultra-light piece with remarkable durability. Originally, Westmancott intended it to sell it to prominent business moguls from the Middle East and Russia.

    4. Zoot Suit – $78,000

    The first piece was sold in New York in 2011. Unlike the rest, the idea behind its design is just beyond anyone’s imaginations. Its style may not impress many because of its exaggerated look; it comprises a high-waist tapering trouser and a loose jacket with padded shoulders. Zoot is currently not worn by anyone.

    3. Dormeuil Vanquish II – $95,319

    Dormeuil Vanquish II is made by a French textile company. The company is widely known for its most expensive men's suits. However, this one is different because it was specifically made for the rich such as presidents, celebrities, and kings.

    The fabrics used to make it are primarily the reason for its hefty price. Their quality and availability make it difficult for many tailors to acquire. The fabrics are as follows: Fifteen Point Eight, Ambassador, Kirgzy White, royal Qivuik, and Dorsilk.

    2. Alexander Amosu Vanquish II Bespoke – $101,860

    This garment was made shortly after Amosu established his clothing line in 2019. Unlike the rest, it is primarily made of two most expensive fabric wool, qivuik and vicuna. Also, its nine buttons contain 18-carat diamond and gold.

    It took the designers more than 800 working hours and approximately 5000 stitches to be ready. The buyer of this piece is yet to be identified. It was rumoured that it was transported to him by an armoured vehicle.

    1. Stuart Hughes Diamond Edition – $781,979

    So, how much is the most expensive suit? Well, this Stuart Hughes Diamond Edition sells at a whopping £599,000.00. This piece was made by two of the world’s most experienced designers, Stuart Hughes and Richard Jewels. However, Stuart was the owner of the idea.

    The piece is made from high-quality wool, silk, cashmere, and diamonds. Roughly, the garment contains 480 half-carat diamonds in the interior lining of the jacket. It took them 800 working hours to put together everything.
