    Holly Hassel is the new champion of Jeopardy who faced Neilay Amin, the new contestant and six-day champion Suresh Krishnan. The game had to undergo many changes, with Hassel winning the total amount of $10,500. Despite Krishnan being the six-day champion, he was placed second with a total amount of $5,200, and the third contestant, Amin, ended up with $799. The show was originally taped on 4th April in Culver City, California, and on 14th June 2023, Holly Hassel went on a date with her 25 friends, watching out for her performance on the stage.

    Early Life

    Holly Hassel (47) completed her high school at Brainerd High School (1990 to 1992). She received her degree in B.A. and M.A. in English from the same university – St. Cloud State University. Holly later received her Ph.D. from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln (1997 to 2002). She conversationally speaks Spanish and English.

    Holly Hassel Career

    Holly Hassel has been working as an English professor for 21 years and continues her job with the University of Wisconsin-Marathon County. She is the professor of English and Chair of the UW Colleges Women’s Studies Program.

    Jeopardy Journey

    Suresh Krishnan was in the lead, and all of a sudden, the day when Hassel came, she tightened her grip as the daily double round started. First, she wagered $1,600 and then started giving the correct answers responding correctly to every clue, and then came in the lead. After that, when the Double Jeopardy round started, she had $5,200 and was still in the lead against the two contestants – Krishnan and Amin.
