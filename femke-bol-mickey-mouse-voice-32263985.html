    Fans were left wondering if Disney's Mickey Mouse had smashed a world record after hearing a star athlete’s post-race interview.

    Dutch sensation Femke Bol stormed to gold in the 400m at the World Indoor Championships in Glasgow, setting a new world best of 49.17 seconds in the process. Bol’s career is going from strength to strength after she won gold in both the 400m hurdles and the 4x400m relay at the outdoor World Championships in Budapest last year.

    Following her latest success, the 24-year-old gave a delightful interview to the BBC, with many fans likening her voice to that of the legendary cartoon mouse.

    Meanwhile, on the final day of competition in Glasgow, Great Britain set a new national record as they qualified for the final of the 4x400m relay.

    Lina Nielsen, Ama Pipi, Hannah Kelly and Jessie Knight won their heat in 3:26.4 seconds in Glasgow. They finished almost a second ahead of Jamaica, with the Czech Republic third. Bol and the Netherlands, the favourites for the gold medal, qualified from the first heat in 3:27.70.
