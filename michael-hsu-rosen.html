    Michael Hsu Rosen is a talented American actor and dancer.  Michael Hsu Rosen is known for his roles as  Jayden and Nabil in the Netflix series “Pretty Smart” and “Tiny Pretty Things.”

    Early Life

Currently, he is 32 years old as of 2024 and according to the astrological chart, he has a birth sign of Gemini. He holds a US citizenship and belongs to Caucasian-Chinese ethnicity. Additionally, he follows the Christian religion.

    He has not yet shared much information regarding his parents, siblings, and childhood life. However, according online website, as a child, he was interested in acting and dancing.     Michael Hsu Rosen – Education

    Moving on to his academics, he attended Ethical Culture Fieldston School. Later, he went to the Professional Children’s School as well as the School of American Ballet (for 9 years). In addition, he enrolled at Yale University but after five semesters, he dropped out of college.

    Professional Career

    Michael Hsu Rosen was only 12 years old when he was cast as a Child Dancer in the short film “The Empty Building.” Moreover, he has been a Broadway star since he was 17 years old when he had his first professional audition for the role of Chino in “West Side Story.” It was not only his first major audition but also his first time singing in public. He later made his Broadway debut as Chino in a dance-heavy revival of West Side Story in 2009.

    He made his debut as an actor in the 2012 short drama film “Food of Love” playing the role of Oscar and it was directed by Sarah Rosen. Moreover, he starred in an episode of the French-American action-comedy police TV series “Taxi Brooklyn” in 2014.

    He returned to the stage starring in the world premiere of Matthew Lopez’s at the Hartford stage. Additionally, he was again seen at Lincoln Centre Theater performing the role of Nicholas Magallanes at the world premiere of Richard Nelson Nikolai. In the 2016 TV movie “Looking” he was cast as Jimmy.

    Recent Works

    In 2019, he was seen playing the role of Andy in the short film “Lavender” as well as playing the role of Deacon in the comedy-drama film “After Class” the same year.  He was seen in two episodes of the TV series “Jessica Jones” playing the role of Laurent Lyonne.
