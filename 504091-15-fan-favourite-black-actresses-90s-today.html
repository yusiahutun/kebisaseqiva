    Who were some of the biggest Hollywood stars of the 1990s? The were many, without a doubt. The era saw the emergence of top talents, including some talented actors from the black community. Despite facing challenges and obstacles related to representation and opportunity, these stars rose to become the best in the niche. So, who are they? And who are the fan-favourite black actresses from the 90s?

    Top black actresses from the 90s include some of the top names in the entertainment industry. These stars lit up cinemas with their acting skills gaining massive followers. In addition, these actresses have used their fame to advocate for change and equality, paving the way for future generations of black actors.

    15 fan-favourite black actresses from the 90s

    Beautiful black actresses from the 90s include top names like Halle Berry and Jada Pinkett Smith. These stars were the go-to performers in an era that had some iconic and established names like Whoopi Goldberg and Angela Bassett. So, who else was a fan favourite in the 1990s?

    According to IMDb, here is a list of the top 15 black movie stars of the 90s.

    1. Halle Berry

    Halle Maria Berry is among the most famous black female actresses ever. Some of her movie roles include The Flintstones (1994), Strictly Business (1991) and Catwoman (2004). Though she received worldwide recognition in the early 2000s, Halle Berry's breakthrough was in the 1990s.

    Halle has received various accolades, including the Academy, Primetime Emmy, and Golden Globe Awards.

    2. Angela Bassett

    Angela Evelyn Bassett is among the top black actresses from the '90s TV shows. Her notable TV roles include The Flash (episode: Beat the Clock) and One Special Victory. She has also appeared in films like What's Love Got to Do with It (1993) and Panther (1993). Angela is the recipient of various accolades, including two Golden Globe Awards.

    3. Jada Pinkett Smith

    Jada Koren Pinket Smith is an actress, singer, talk show host and businesswoman. She rose to prominence in 1991 during the sitcom A Different World as Lena James. Jada has received various accolades, including the NAACP Image Awards and the MTV Movie & TV Awards.

    4. Queen Latifah

    Queen Latifah, Dana Elaine Owens, is a singer-songwriter, rapper and actress. She is among the top black actress from the 1990s featuring in film and TV shows. Some of her notable roles include Jungle Fever (1991), Set It Off (1996) and The Bone Collector (1999).

    5. Jenifer Lewis

    Jenifer Jeanette Lewis is an American singer and actress. She has been in the entertainment industry for over 40 years. Jenifer began her career in Broadway musicals before appearing in the comedy-drama Beaches (1988) and Sister Act (1992).

    6. Lynn Whitfield

    If you were a fan of the 1991 HBO biographical film, The Josephine Baker Story, you must have encountered Lynn Whitfield. She is an American actress who played the film's lead character Freda Josephine Baker.

    Lynn Whitfield has been active in the TV and film industry since 1977 and has received various accolades in her 45-year-long career.

    7. Nia Long

    Nia Talita Long is an American actress known for her role as Beullah "Lisa" Wilkes in the 1991 sitcom, The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air. She has appeared in other flicks, including Boyz n the Hood (1991), Friday (1995) and Big Momma's House (2000).

    8. Vivica A Fox

    Vivica Anjanetta Fox is an American producer, host and actress. She has featured in various films, the most notable being Independence Day (1996), Set It Off (1996) and Soul Food (1997).

    Vivica Fox has various accolades, including the MTV Movie Awards, American Black Film Festival and the CinemaCon Award.

    9. Vannessa Williams

    Vannesa Lynn Williams is an American singer, dancer and actress. She was the first African-American recipient of the Miss American title in 1984. Vanessa made her debut on TV on a 1984 episode of The Love Boat.

    Her other notable works include featuring in various episodes of The Jacksons: An American Dream and The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air. Vannessa has also appeared in other flicks from the 1990s, including Soul Food, Light It Up and Hoodlum.

    10. Regina King

    Regina Rene King is an American director, producer and actress. She rose to fame for her role as Brenda Jenkins in the TV series 227 and the 1995 film Friday (as Dana Jones). Regina has received various accolades, including four Primetime Emmy Awards, an Academy Award and a Golden Globe Award.

    11. Meagan Good

    Meagan Monique Good is an American actress known for appearing in various films and TV shows. Her breakout role came in 1997 when she featured in the American Southern Gothic film Eve's Bayou.

    12. Monica Calhoun

    Monica Calhoun is among the top young black actresses from the '90s still active in 2023. She is an American film and television actress best known for her roles in The Best Man, Bagdad Cafe, and The Players Club, among others.

    Calhoun has been active since 1985 and has received two nominations for an Emmy Award and one for the NAACP Image Award.

    13. Nicole Ari Parker

    Nicole Ari Parker Kodjoe is an actress and model from the United States known for her roles in the comedy flicks Boogie Nights (1997) and Blue Streak (1999). Her role in the Soul Food drama series earned her five nominations for the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actress in a Drama Series.

    14. Terri Juanita Vaughn

    Terri Juanita Vaughn is an American actor, director and producer known for her role in The Steve Harvey Show (1997-2002). Terri J Vaughn's films include Don't Be a Menace to South Central While Drinking Your Juice in the Hood and Friday.

    Terri has various accolades, including the NAACP Image Awards for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series.

    15. Wendy Raquel Robinson

    Wendy Robinson is an actress from the United States known for her role in The Steve Harvey Show (1996) and The Game (2006). She made her acting debut in 1993 on the Fox TV series Martin. Wendy's other notable roles include in 1995 flick The Walking Dead, Ringmaster (1998) and MANTIS (1994).
