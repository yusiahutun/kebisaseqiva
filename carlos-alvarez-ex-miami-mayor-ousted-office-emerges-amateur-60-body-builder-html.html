    Former Mayor Carlos Alvarez - who was recalled from office two years ago and hasn't been seen since - recently re-emerged as an over-60 bodybuilding champion.

    Photos of the Miami-Dade Mayor emerged late Tuesday showing the 60-year-old as he has never been seen before - wearing tiny black briefs with his bulging, tattooed muscles all the way from his pecs to his calves covered in tanning lotion.

    Alvarez, a twice divorced father of three, took home first prize in the National Physique Committee's South Florida 'Over 60s' Master's bodybuilding competition at Miami James L. Knight Center.

    As Mayor:Alvarez was ousted from office on March 11, 2011, in the largest recall vote of a local official in U.S. history after constituents fought back against a property tax hike and increased county employee salaries

    Event promoter Sergio Pacheco said Alvarez’s victory over five other men qualified him for the more prestigious Junior National Master’s competition.

    Alvarez was ousted from office on March 11, 2011, in the largest recall vote of a local official in U.S. history - 88 per cent - after constituents fought back against a property tax hike and increased county employee salaries.

    He was mayor for seven-and-a-half years and at one time was the most powerful men in Miami-Dade County.

    Since his humiliating departure, he has rarely been seen in public.

    He has been frequently spotted at a tony gym at Merrick Park in Coral Gables.

    Deanna Clevesy, a spokeswoman for Equinox Gym Coral Gables, confirmed to the Miami Herald that Alvarez works out there, but refused to share details on his regimen.

    A judge at the November 24 bodybuilding competition said: 'One of the other judges who lives in Miami pointed out to me [who he was] but Alvarez didn't broadcast it'

    Alvarez. a twice divorced father of three, has barely been seen for the past two years. But he has been spotted working out at Equinox gym in Coral Gables

    Michael Sansevero photographed the event. He said Alvarez paid $75 for pictures and video.

    'I was kind of surprised when I saw him,' Sansevero said. 'He must always have been in good shape, but he was in real good shape.'

    In his body-building bio, there is no mention of his time as mayor, but it does say he was the former police director.

    A judge at the November 24 bodybuilding competition told the Herald: 'One of the other judges who lives in Miami pointed out to me [who he was] but Alvarez didn't broadcast it.'

    When the former mayor left office, he was earning salary and benefits worth $325,309, county records show. His last financial disclosure put his net worth at $1.74 million.

    Alvarez’s annual police pension pays him $180,216, and he received a one-time payout of $287,879 for entering an early retirement plan.
