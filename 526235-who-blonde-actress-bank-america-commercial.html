    Early childhood 

    Elizabeth Banks was born on 10th February 1974 in Pittsfield, Massachusetts. Her parents are Ann Wallace and Mark P. Mitchell. Her father is a Vietnam War veteran who got to work for a car manufacturer (General Electric) upon discharge, while her mother was a banker.

    She attended Pittsfield High School, where she started acting aside from engaging in baseball and horseback riding. She became a member of the Massachusetts Junior Classical League and, upon graduation, transferred to the University of Pennsylvania.

    At the institution, she joined the Delta Delta Delta Sorority and got elected to the Friars Senior Society. Elizabeth graduated from Magna Cumlaude in 1996 with a major in communications and a minor in theatre arts.

    Although Elizabeth was raised Christian, she converted to Judaism upon marrying her husband, Max Handelman, and has been practising the religion for over 15 years.

    Acting career

    After her graduation, Elizabeth joined the Screen Actors Guild and debuted in the film Surrender Dorothy. However, she had to change her name to Elizabeth Banks from Elizabeth Mitchell to avoid legalities owing to an existing actress under the alias registered to the Screen Actors Guild.
