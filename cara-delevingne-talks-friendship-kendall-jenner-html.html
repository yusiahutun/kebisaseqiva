    Cara Delevingne talks about her friendship withKendall Jenner

    They previouslymerged their monikersto dub themselves CaKe and even made merchandise emblazoned with their nickname in the past. AndCara Delevingne has spoke about her friendship with Kendall Jenner in a new candid interview with the UK edition of ELLE magazine.

    03 Jun 2024, 00:00

    They previously merged their monikers to dub themselves CaKe and even made merchandise emblazoned with their nickname in the past. 

    And Cara Delevingne has spoke about her friendship with Kendall Jenner in a new candid interview with the UK edition of ELLE magazine.

    The model turned actress, 27, revealed how they first became pals in the modelling industry and how she had 'low expectations for friends' as they didn't even talk at first.

    Friendships: Cara Delevingne has spoke about her friendship with Kendall Jenner in a new candid interview with the UK edition of ELLE magazine 

    She said: 'Honestly, I had low expectations for friends in this industry because: a) I didn’t think people would want to be my friend, and b) I didn’t know if making friends through this process would be fickle or seem not real. So I was pleasantly surprised!'

    Cara added: 'I didn’t meet Kendall until I had been modelling for a bit. We had done a few shows together and didn’t say a word to each other. Eventually, we started talking and I was like, "Oh, you’re alright. We should hang out after this."

    'She was like, "Alright, cool." It was very easy. I found that you automatically know if someone is trying to make an effort that isn’t real, and when a friendship seems contrived.'

    The pair have been firm friends for several years and even concocted a plan to flog clothes in 2016 baring their CaKe logo.

    BFFs: The model turned actress, 27, revealed how they first became pals in the modelling industry and how she had 'low expectations for friends' as they didn't even talk at first (pictured in December 2014) 

    CaKe: They previously merged their monikers to dub themselves CaKe and even made merchandise emblazoned with their nickname in the past (pictured in 2015) 

    They reportedly fell out after Kendall's momager Kris Jenner wanted to trademark ‘CaKe by Kendall and Cara’, putting her daughter's name first.

    Kendall insisted the rumours were false, however, announcing: 'When we went to Glastonbury, we wore those CaKe shirts, but we just got them made at a store down the street! It was never a real thing, just something between us.'

    It comes after Cara stunned on the red carpet for the premiere of her new series Carnival Row last week, where she stars alongside Orlando Bloom.

    'Easy': Speaking about their friendship, she told the publication: 'It was very easy. I found that you automatically know if someone is trying to make an effort that isn’t real, and when a friendship seems contrived.' (pictured in September 2018) 

    At the event, the star insisted that she hasn't turned her back on her first love, modelling.

    When asked if she'll return to the catwalk, she told MailOnline: 'Return? I never left. I don't think you have to pick and choose what you do, especially as a creative person.

    'There are many different ways to express yourself, I don't think you should stop yourself from doing anything.'

    Plans: The pair have been firm friends for several years and even concocted a plan to flog clothes in 2016 baring their CaKe logo (Cara pictured in February) 

    Co-star: It comes after Cara stunned on the red carpet for the premiere of her new series Carnival Row last week, where she stars alongside Orlando Bloom (pictured) 

    On what it was like to work with Orlando and the cast, she said: 'The cast made it, and to be honest the crew, everyone on set. The camaraderie on set, and the passion and the care for every detail.

    'The crew made this what this is. I know that people can't see it but they're there. But Orlando, he's a brilliant actor and it was amazing to work with someone who had the same passion to make this stand out and be something different.'  

    The October issue of ELLE UK is on sale from Thursday 5 September 2019.
