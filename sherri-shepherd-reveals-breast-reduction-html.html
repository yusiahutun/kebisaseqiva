    Sherri Shepherd was over the moon on Monday when she revealed that she had gotten a breast reduction two months earlier.

    The 56-year-old actress announced on her daytime talk show Sherri that she had gotten the surgery on July 13, and not only did she love the new appearance, but it helped clear up pain she had been suffering from her breasts.

    She was bringing her show back for its second season premiere amid the ongoing writers and actors strikes.

    Although some fans have criticized her for not staying off the air in solidarity, she is governed by a different contract that the one affecting striking actors, and her show has never employed writers.

    'I get so many comments about my body and a lot of y'all kept saying, "Sherri, you’re too top heavy." And I would get comments like, "If you just got a boob job everything would be balanced." So guess what? I got my boobs done!' Sherri shouted on her show while showing off her new figure.

    What a difference! Sherri Shepherd, 56, revealed on Monday on Sherri that she had gotten a breast reduction on July 13. She showed off her new smaller bust and gushed about how much better she felt with the weight literally off her shoulders (seen Monday on the left and in February on the right)

    She looked glamorous in a hot pink dress that highlighted her cleavage.

    Sherri also gave her fans a glimpse of her before and after by posting photos of her pre-breast reduction on the video screen behind her.

    'I had a breast reduction over the summer and season 2, everything is going to be bigger except these boobs! To be clear, I did not get this boob job because of all the comments. I got the boob job because I just wanted to see what it felt like to sleep on my stomach,' she joked.

    She was clearly loving her new look, though. 

    'I’m so happy that I did it,' she gushed, before noting that she had previously had a 42DD cup size.

    She also added that it was a 'bittersweet' feeling, as she had appreciated the former size of her breasts and their appearance, but they had started to impinge on her life. 

    'I thought I was carrying around the weight of the world but really it was the weight of my boobs,' she added with a laugh. 'All jokes aside, they were so heavy I was slouching all the time. It started becoming really painful, my back was hurting very badly.'

    She added that she felt 'better,' and she clarified that she was glad that she waited until this time in her life when it felt needed.

    Having a laugh: 'I had a breast reduction over the summer and season 2, everything is going to be bigger except these boobs!' she joked

    Downsizing: 'I’m so happy that I did it,' she gushed, before noting that she had previously had a 42DD cup size

    Hard to get around: She added that her larger breasts gave her constant back pain and made her slouch, though she was happy that she waited until now; seen in January in NYC

    'God gave them to me, they served me well but now, as I get older, I can sit up straight. I feel lighter,' she said. 

    Sherri compared her reduction to her weight loss in recent years, when she managed to shed around 40 pounds, though she gained back a portion of the weight at the height of the ongoing coronavirus pandemic.

    Elsewhere in the show, Sherri tried to clear up confusion from fans who worried that she was crossing a picket line to bring her talk show back.

    Although the 30 Rock actress is a member of SAG-AFTRA, which is currently striking against major studios and streamers as it fights for better pay, larger residuals and guarantees that actors will not be replaced with AI, her daytime talk show is governed by a different contract.

    She also doesn't fall under the WGA strike, as her show never had writers. 

    'This summer you all may have seen your favorite actors and Hollywood stars have been on the picket lines with the SAG-AFTRA and WGA strikes,' she began.

    'There has been so much confusion about who can work and who can’t work … The Sherri show is not a WGA show and we have never employed WGA writers, so us coming back to work isn’t crossing the picket line,' she continued.

    Her return was recently caught up in the drama over Drew Barrymore and Bill Maher's decisions to bring back their talk shows sans writers.

    The move swept up an enormous amount of criticism, and not just from members of the writers guild, but also from fans on social media.

    Barrymore took the brunt of the criticism, which was not helped by a somber video she posted in which she attempted to explain her decision to bring back the show rather than waiting out the strike.

    Clearing things up: She also addressed criticism of her return to TV after Drew Barrymore were shamed for bring back their shows and ultimately reversed course

    In the clear: Sherri is in SAG-AFTRA but is under a different contract, and her show has never employeed WGA covered writers; seen in May 2022 in NYC

    It was shortly deleted after backlash, and she subsequently announced that plans to return had been put on hold until after the strike was resolved.

    Bill Maher also announced that he was holding off on bring back HBO's Real Time shortly afterward, and he claimed his change of heart was because of reports that the WGA and the AMPTP, which represents the studios, were returning to the bargaining table.

    However, Sherri has made it clear that she supports both the writers and actors strikes when she posted videos to Instagram earlier this month showing herself on the picket line with SAG-AFTRA members. 

    'Actors & writers are now fighting against a corporate regime who literallly wishes us to be replaced by AI. They refuse to negotiate in good faith & want to see us broken and not be paid fair wages for our hard work. This is unacceptable. And this is what my fellow @wga colleagues and actors are fighting for,' she wrote.
