    Epsom, New Hampshire

    Getaway

    Camping has never looked so cozy. Cuddle up to your love in this adorable Getaway Blake Brook cabin, located in the peaceful New Hampshire wilderness—just 1.5 hours from Boston. Go for a hike in Epsom Town Forest, take a drive into the state's capital city to dine at local favorite The Barley House, or stay in and enjoy some campfire treats. The best part? You won't be totally off the grid. The beautiful digs come complete with air conditioning, heat, and a landline to contact the Getaway team should you need anything.

    Barnard, Vermont

    Twin Farms

    Tucked away in the Vermont countryside, The Aviary at Twin Farms is a must-visit for any couple. The private cottage—just one of 10 at the resort—was designed for endless romance. With two stories and a lookout spot to take in all the gorgeous scenery, you and your special someone will never want to leave. The standout feature is the dreamy indoor tub and fireplace combo. If you do happen to venture outside, try the rustic Barnard Inn Restaurant for delicious farm-to-table dishes.

    North Woodstock, New Hampshire

    Cafe Lafayette Dinner Train

    All aboard! The Cafe Lafayette Dinner Train—operating in the spring, summer, and fall months (but book ahead!)—is an experience unlike any other. Not only will you and your date be treated to a delicious five-course dinner, but you'll also take a two-hour ride around the White Mountains of New Hampshire. The views of the stunning Pemi River Valley are enough to make anyone feel the love. Extend your stay at one of the area's many inviting hotels, like the The Village of Loon Mountain.

    BOOK NOW

    Woodstock, Vermont

    Jess Notargiacomo

    We'd never tell you not to visit Vermont in the fall (the leaves are gorgeous), but there's an irresistible charm in the winter too. For some tranquil time together, look no further than The Spa at the Woodstock Inn. There are both indoor and outdoor experiences, ranging from a healing hot tub to a lilac meditation tree. If it's cold outside, you can snuggle by the fire while waiting for your couples massage. Once you're feeling rejuvenated, go for dinner at The Prince & The Pauper, known for its creative cocktails.

    BOOK NOW

    Newport, Rhode Island

    Michael Frederic Gillespie

    This town is known for its summertime fun, but the Turret Suite at the Castle Hill Inn can be enjoyed year-round. It offers 300-plus-degree views of the famous Rhode Island coastline. So crack open the Champagne, add some bubbles to the tub, light some candles, and let the romance begin. (Just make sure you don't book during its month-long winter hiatus!) Nearby, there are plenty of activities and good eats to enjoy: From fine dining at Bouchard Restaurant to the famous Cliff Walk, you won't be bored.

    BOOK NOW

    Portland, Maine

    Read McKendree

    If you and your better half are into history, Blind Tiger is as good as it gets. Originally built in 1823, this guest house was used as a secret gathering place during the Prohibition Era. Everything from the hidden billiards room to the complimentary breakfast creates a welcoming atmosphere. The cutest hotel amenity? A packed picnic basket and blanket for a date night in one of the nearby parks! Pro tip: Grab brunch at Hot Suppa, which offers Southern classics and unique takes on comfort dishes, like "Scrambalaya."

    BOOK NOW

    Simsbury, Connecticut

    Meg Mason

    When you think of romance, you think of dinner and a view. Lucky for you, Millwright's Restaurant offers that (the hanging flowers and waterfall are stunning!) and more. With dishes like chili garlic shrimp and beef tartare, it's almost a guarantee that you'll leave feeling full—and happy. There's also a wide range of beautiful places to stay and fun activities nearby, from The Simsbury 1820 House to The Old Drake Hill Flower Bridge.

    BOOK NOW

    Bar Harbor, Maine

    Taylor Watts Photography

    For a full "glamping" getaway, grab your sweetheart and head to Terramor Outdoor Resort. Located in the heart of Mount Desert Island, the two of you can reconnect with nature...without feeling like you're on an episode of Survivor. Tents come with WiFi, private bathrooms, and electricity. Venture out to the nearby Acadia National Park for an Insta-worthy hike, then enjoy Maine Hour (the resort's version of a happy hour, complete with a campfire and signature cozy cocktails). Treat yourself to a famous Maine lobster at Bar Harbor Lobster Bakes for dinner.

    BOOK NOW

    Lenox, Massachusetts

    AGF//Getty Images

    Live out your Bridgerton dreams at this historical paradise. The Mount is a National Historic Landmark, first built by Gilded Age novelist Edith Wharton in 1902. Beginning every May, guests can stroll through the spacious Italian Garden, filled with fountains, porticos, and flowered pathways. Continue your lovey-dovey getaway at the quaint Brook Farm Inn, then order French cuisine at nearby hotspot Zinc Bistro & Bar.

    BOOK NOW

    Harwich, Massachusetts

    Wequassett Resort and Golf Club

    Come for the waterside view, stay for the food at Twenty-Eight Atlantic, located in the Wequassett Resort and Golf Club. As Cape Cod's first and only Forbes Five-Star restaurant in the region, you'll get to dine on a coastal menu by Executive Chef James Hackney and sip from an award-winning wine list. While the resort takes a brief winter hiatus in January, reservations reopen in February—just in time for Valentine's Day! Spend the rest of your stay visiting nearby attractions, like the Nines Arts Gallery and Cape Cod Lavender Farm.

    BOOK NOW

    Cambridge, Vermont

    Education Images//Getty Images

    What's a little romance without some wine? Boyden Valley Winery is run out of a 1875 carriage barn and incorporates ingredients like freshly picked apples and 100% pure maple syrup. Every July, the fourth-generation family farm opens for business and welcomes guests to try its famous Vermont Ice Cream Liqueurs and Maple Bourbon. Speaking of ice cream, the Ben & Jerry's Factory is under an hour away and offers tours, and a 15-minute from there you'll find another one of our top spots: Stowe, Vermont. (Get the best of both worlds and spend the night there after your activities!).

    BOOK NOW

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    Stowe, Vermont

    Sam Trombino / EyeEm//Getty Images

    Though you can't go wrong visiting when the leaves are changing colors in the fall or for a ski trip in the winter, the Stowe Balloon Festival occurs every July. Watch as the balloons take off at sunrise and fly high over the bright Vermont scenery, then go for dinner at one of the many nearby restaurants. Named the top ski town for Foodies in the United States by Fodor’s Travel, there's a cuisine for every taste! If you're looking for a place to stay, we recommend the popular (and cozy!) Lodge at Spruce Peak.

    BOOK NOW

    Wolfeboro, New Hampshire

    Amy Barnard

    If you're looking for a vacation that you'll be talking about for years to come, consider staying at the Pickering House Inn. The charming estate was once a local landmark known as "The Yellow House" that was built in 1813. Located within walking distance of Lake Winnipesaukee, there's plenty to do and eat nearby. See a movie at the Weirs Drive-In Theater or enjoy freshly baked goods at the Cup & Crumb coffee shop. Bonus fact: Pickering House Inn was ranked #1 Best Resort Hotel in the U.S. and #1 in New England in the 2022 Travel + Leisure World's Best Awards. Only the best for your boo!

    BOOK NOW

    Stockbridge, Massachusetts

    JOSEPH PREZIOSO//Getty Images

    What's more romantic than strolling hand in hand through a glittering forest? Thousands of colored lights are used each year for the famous Naumkeag Winterlights event, open from the end of November through the beginning of January. The magical garden property illuminates the night as you make your way through the magical path. Don't forget to take sweet holiday photos along the way! Spend the rest of your vacay snuggled up in a suite at Wheatleigh, a nearby luxury hotel with fine dining and a huge wine cellar.

    BOOK NOW

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    Kennebunkport, Maine

    Earth at Hidden Pond

    Looking for a dinner spot with a romantic ambience? Earth at Hidden Pond is known for its dark wooden decor and warm lighting—the walls were built with the actual trees cut down to make room for the restaurant! The farm-to-table dishes only add to its charm, with ingredients picked daily from on-site organic gardens. For your post-dinner stay, book a room at the gorgeous Nonantum Resort, which offers easy access to Dock Square's shops and restaurants.

    BOOK NOW

    Boston, Massachusetts

    Boston Globe//Getty Images

    It might be called the Emerald Necklace, but it's a long way from Oz. Made up of seven miles (and 1,100 acres!) of intersecting parks, this impressive conservatory has plenty to see. (Yes, Boston is more than a bustling city!) You and your date can rent a rowboat at Jamaica Pond, or hike to Peters Hill—the highest point in the area—for the ultimate view of the the city skyline. Afterwards, visit one Boston's countless hotels and restaurants, like The Godfrey Hotel and Spoke Wine Bar.

    BOOK NOW

    Morris, Connecticut

    Winvian Farm

    Couples massage by the fire? Yes, please. You'll leave Winvian Farm Spa feeling more refreshed and ready for romance than ever before. Stop by the reflecting pool for tranquil conversation, take a private yoga class together, or hit the Meditation Lounge with a mug of organic tea. We're relaxed just thinking about it. After, stop by the Restaurant at Winvian Farm for even more great—and delicious—memories.

    BOOK NOW

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    Lincoln, New Hampshire

    Kirkikis//Getty Images

    Adventurous couples will love exploring the Flume George at Franconia State Park in New Hampshire. It's an 800-foot natural gorge that allows adrenaline junkies to live out their inner Indiana Jones fantasies. The jaw-dropping structure first began forming 200 million years ago during the Jurassic Period! All that exercise will make you hungry, so grab a meal at La Vista Italian Cuisine at RiverWalk Resort at Loon Mountain. The hotel also has its own winery and ice skating rink!

    BOOK NOW

    West Hartford, Connecticut

    LeenaDamle//Getty Images

    It's a known fact that roses and romance go together. By the time you leave Elizabeth Park, you'll be absolutely head over heels for one another. The elegant flower arches—along with the endless other displays throughout the gardens—are something out of a dream. May we then suggest The Goodwin Hotel for your stay (you'll love its classic stone fireplaces and warm mahogany woods) and Division West Bistro and Bar for dinner.

    BOOK NOW
