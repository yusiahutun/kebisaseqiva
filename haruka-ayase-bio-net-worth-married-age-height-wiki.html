    Haruka Ayase Early Life

    Born as Aya Tademaru on 24th March 1985, in Hiroshima, Haruka is the elder sister of her brother. Haruka was active in sports and played basketball in middle school. She also competed in the Chugoku region Ekiden tournament(long-distance relay race) during junior high school.

    During her freshman year, Haruka auditioned for the 25th Horipro Talent Scout Caravan and pursued her career. Later, she moved to Tokyo city where she frequently worked out to get an actress’ body and lost 6 kilos.

    Haruka Ayase Career, Movies, & Drama

    Firstly, Haruka starter her career as a gravure idol then branched off into acting in small roles. In 2003, she acted in Fuji TV drama Boku no Ikiru Michi (The Way I Live) which made a positive impression on the public. Along with that, Haruka performed as Hirose Aki in TBS drama Crying out Love, in the center of the world where she beat 723 contestants.

    Haruko has become popular by working on the big screen acting as Cyborg Girl, Happy Flight, and Oppai Volleyball. Ayase played as a cyborg in Cyborg girl, an airline stewardess in Happy Flight and a volleyball coach in Oppai Volleyball. Along with that, Ayase played a blind swordsman in Ichi.

    From 2010 to 2014, Haruka acted in several films like The Incite Mill, Princess Toyotomi, Hotaru no Hikari, Himitsu no Akko-chan, Real and All-Round Appraiser. Then, Haruka starred in the drama Our Little Sister which led her to win the Best Actress Award at the 37th Yokohama Film Festival. Later Ayase also starred in The Kodai Family.

    However, her biggest hit till date is the movie Honnoji Hotel (2017) directed by Masayuki Suzuki. In 2018, Ayase played the lead role in the fantasy film Tonight, at the Movies.

    In addition to her acting career, Ayase also has a successful music career. She released her first debut single Period in 2006. It went straight to Number.8 on the weekly Oricon Singles Chart. Consequently, in 2006 and 2007, she released two other singles “Kosaten Days” and “Hikoki Gumo”.
