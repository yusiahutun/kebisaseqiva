    15 must-have African hair accessories for black women

    Afro hair requires proper maintenance to look and feel good regardless of the plaiting style. Using appropriate African hair accessories is one way to enhance your looks. Luckily, black women have several options that they can use to make their hair look better.

    06 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Afro hair requires proper maintenance to look and feel good regardless of the plaiting style. Using appropriate African hair accessories is one way to enhance your looks. Luckily, black women have several options that they can use to make their hair look better.

    Hair accessory selections vary considerably in sizes, shapes, colours, and purposes. These tools simplify hair routines that are sometimes cumbersome and time-consuming. While some of these accessories are good for styling the hair, others give it a different impression altogether.

    African hair accessories- Full guide on hair accessories for afro hair

    Having the right tools is the only way of keeping your hair at its best without messing with your looks. Daily hair routines require at least one of these black girl hair accessories for a comfortable styling experience. Therefore, your investment in every one of these modern or ethnic hair accessories is worth it.

    1. Headscarf

    Read also

    Styling your hair for the night is unnecessary because you will not be looking after it in your sleep. Take a step and acquire a headscarf for the safety of your afro hair. The accessory makes it easier to handle your hair at night without exposing it to abrasion.

    Most importantly, the headscarf prevents your hair from drying by retaining its oils, which may spill to the beddings. Formation of frizzes is not an option with this head covering on at night or when actively going about your daily routines.

    Besides maintaining your hair neatness, a headscarf is reliable in protecting sensitive hairstyles such as curls from possible damages. A perfectly designed headscarf or headwrap makes a great choice when it comes to black hair accessories for weddings

    2. Cuffs

    Many people prefer golden hair cuffs on their afro hair because of their dazzling looks. These styling tools are appropriate for almost every hairstyle, including curls, twists, and braids. Their affordability and beauty are all you need to rock your favourite hairstyle.

    Choose the right size depending on the thickness of your dreads. You will never go wrong with cuffs as your black hair accessories for braids.

    3. Jaw clips

    Those with long hair require jaw clips to keep it in place and avoid messy looks. The clip is a good alternative if you do not have a hair tie. Jaw clips come in different styles, colours, and sizes, depending on your preference.

    These afro hair clips are useful for keeping voluminous hair out of the way by twisting and giving it a firm lock. It is also a helpful accessory when you're in the shower and do not want to wet your hair.

    4. Hair pins

    Pins are excellent for securing your hair, just like jaw clips but are more applicable on small hair volumes. Those with buns often use them to secure the hairstyle. The good part is that hairpins are formal and, therefore, versatile in their application.

    These useful hair accessories are appropriate for all woman. Bobby pins are not only useful in keeping your hair in place but also when giving it a shiny look. Their bright looks blend perfectly with the dark theme that leaves afro hair looking shiny and attractive.

    5. Hair barrettes

    Clipping the hair after gathering and fastening requires a barrette for a stylish impression. There are different barrette variations from which you can pick. The most common is a bow barrette that is both stylish and decorative.

    The barrette colour is equally of interest when it comes to killer looks. It is one of the perfect hair accessories for natural African American hair.

    6. Afro hair elastics

    Elastics are simpler and more affordable hair accessories that guarantee good looks. They give the hair a decorative feel without exaggerating the touch.

    Hair professionals do not classify rubber bands as part of hair elastics because of the damage it causes to your hair. Only fabric-coated rubbers are suitable for use on your hair.

    7. Headpieces

    These black-owned hair accessories give your plain hair a new feel. It also goes a long way in displaying your classy style in its simplest form. Headpieces are often an attachment pegged to overlay accessories such as headbands.

    Styling the headpieces is straightforward as long as you get the right colour and size. Start by identifying the right spot on your hair, then fix the piece gently.

    8. Natural hair headbands and wraps

    These hair tools are always in fashion and a convenient way to add some accessories around your head. They come in variations to suit different hair, colours, and styles. For instance, there are thick ones and then ultra-tiny band pieces.

    Afro hair bands' robustness makes them one of the most appropriate hair accessories for short natural hair, wavy, and straight variations. Furthermore, those with naturally curly hair can try headwraps when running short of time to style their hair for any occasion. There are different colours and styles of head wraps that anyone can experiment with depending on the plaiting type.

    9. Afro hair ponytail clips

    These clips are a solution for those with voluminous afro hair. Like jaw clips, ponytail clips keep the hair in place and enhance your looks. Even better, they apply to low buns, locs, twists, and braids.

    More so, ponytail clips give the hair a natural feel while at the same time fastening it from spreading over the face.

    10. Wooden beads

    These accessories are reliable for those seeking to spruce up their hair or style it straight. Wooden beads from the 90s are slowly coming back with a new glory that blends perfectly into modern hairstyles. The good thing about these beads is that one can achieve multiple impressions based on styling choice.

    11. Hair sticks

    Most afro hairstyles look perfect with hair sticks on. These are a top hair accessory for those interested to leave an impact with their hairdo. Inserting the stick adds a desirable crystal appearance at the edges.

    The stick is equally useful when out to decorate afro hair buns without irritating the skin. Notice that sticks are better on voluminous hair as opposed to short hair.

    12. Afro hair snap and scrunchies

    These highly affordable hair accessories from the 90s are back again with sleeker designs that merit modern-day use. Snaps and scrunches are convenient for both short and long hair. Most importantly, the two are usable alongside other accessories without exaggerating the looks.

    Afro hair is often vulnerable to apparent damages such as breakages caused by friction and prolonged exposure to heat. Both snaps and scrunchies can help overcome this challenge by keeping off creases. Their rough edges guarantee a firm grip and certainly good looks.

    13. Baseball hat and caps

    Baseball hats have been in use for years but are still fashionable. They are suitable for protecting curls and keeping them healthy. The narrow opening at the back makes it possible to keep the hair in place without additional hair retailer accessories.

    On the other hand, Afro hair caps are an excellent way of keeping yourself warm during cold seasons without compromising the looks. Moreover, they protect the hair from dust and breakage when working. Satin lined caps and hats are particularly preferable because they effectively keep the hair moisturized and free from frizz.

    14. Brass hairpins

    These authentic African hair accessories are an excellent option for amazing looks. Traditional women often rocked this compliment magnet hair accessory with beads or dyes.

    The shiny golden colour of these pins gives them an irresistibly conspicuous look. Both handmade and off-the-shelf Afrocentric brass hairpins are a great choice, especially for plain hairstyles.

    15. Hairbows

    Afro hair bows are getting better by the day and are now a reliable way to keep your hair in check. Bows give your afro hair a deserved finish touch regardless of the hairstyle. You can put on velvet or African bow for any occasion. Always choose the colour that works perfectly for your skin tone and hair colour to avoid appearing too bright.
