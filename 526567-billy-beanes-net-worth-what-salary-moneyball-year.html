    Front office career

    After playing in minor league for a while, Billy approached the team's general manager for a job as an advance scout. He held the position from 1990 to 1993, when he was promoted to assistant manager of the Oakland Athletics. His role was to scout minor-league baseball players.

    On 17 October 1997, he succeeded Sandy Anderson as general manager. Under his leadership, the team reached the playoffs for four consecutive years from 2000 to 2003. In 2002, Oakland Athletics became the first team in over 100 years of American League baseball to win 20 consecutive games.

    On 15 April 2005, his contract was extended to remain with the team as its general manager through 2012. In addition, Lewis Wolff, the new team owner, awarded Billy a small portion of the team's ownership. In February 2012, his contract was extended through 2019.

    On 5 October 2015, Billy was promoted to executive vice president of baseball operations. In November 2022, after seven years of service, he transitioned to senior advisor of Athletics owner John Fisher.
