    The white button-down is the hardest-working top in your closet. The timeless piece has unlimited possibilities when it comes to styling. Pair it with black trousers for a work-appropriate look, or transition to an off-duty vibe with jeans or shorts when the weather warms up. Just take inspiration from style icons like Carolina Herrera who closes almost every runway show in a white button-down tucked into a skirt. “When I don't know what to wear, it's what I choose,” Herrera once said of the staple. “I find it fascinating; it makes me feel secure.”

    Among celebrities, this simple silhouette has also found a long list of fans. “Whether worn in a classic way with Levis leaving an airport or as a dress with heels,” says stylist Emily Evans. “From Audrey Hepburn to Gwyneth Paltrow, and currently Hailey Bieber.” Because you can expect this shirt to be in constant rotation, it’s worth splurging on high-quality materials and a fit that you’ll be able to style in a myriad of ways. And while preferences will be different for everyone, there is a design meant to address any issues from potential stains for the spill-prone to gaping buttons for those with a fuller chest.

    Best Overall

    AYR The Deep End Shirt

    AYR

    What We Love: The shirt is made from a non-stretch oxford cotton, so you can trust that it won’t lose its shape.

    What We Don't Love: The sizing runs slightly large, according to reviews.

    AYR's The Deep End Shirt easily ranked as our favorite on this list simply for its construction. You'll never have to worry about it losing its shape (even with it being made from 100 percent cotton), regardless of how many times you wash it because it was designed to be non-stretch. Thanks to its slightly loose, yet still structured fit, the shirt lends itself to several ways to style, from tucked into black work pants to thrown casually on with a pair of jeans. Even down to the buttons down the front and at the cuffs, the shirt caters to having a crisp look throughout, never looking ill-fitting on the user. Reviews say the quality is worth the high price tag, but it does run slightly large, so consider sizing down if you prefer a more fitted appearance.

    Size: XS–XL | Material: Oxford cotton | Care: Machine wash, tumble dry low

    Best Silk

    Numi The Simone Button-Up Blouse

    Numi

    What We Love: Despite being made from silk, this shirt resists stains and is machine washable.

    What We Don't Love: The style runs small, according to the brand.

    If you're anything like us, wearing white gives you anxiety — every meal or lipstick application feels risky and has you triple-checking your shirt for stains. Even without a clumsy spill, white tops can show those pesky pit stains over time. That's why Numi's silk shirt is a game changer. Made from a blend of sustainable silk and acetate, the Simone is bluesign-certified stain-repellent and no match for stains of any kind.

    InStyle Senior Commerce Writer Eva Thomas tried the shirt after seeing Kelly Ripa constantly wear it on TV. Wearing it over a four-course dinner and two glasses of wine, she didn't end up with a single stain. Thomas also noticed that the shirt didn't cause her excessive sweating as some other silk tops can do. Add in the fact that the shirt is machine washable, and it's safe to say this top will save you time, money, and stress.

    Size: XS–XL | Material: Acetate, silk | Care: Machine wash

    Best for Work

    M.M. LaFleur The Lagarde Shirt

    M.M. LaFleur

    What We Love: Machine washable, wrinkle, and odor resistant, this shirt will easily take you from meetings to happy hour.

    What We Don't Love: It's made from polyester.

    The Lagarde Shirt stood out for its innovative design details made to withstand the plights of an eight-hour workday — wrinkles, odors, and buttons that pop open. That’s because it’s treated with Polygiene, an anti-odor finish, and the polyester fabric is more wrinkle-resistant, moisture-wicking, and impervious to shrinking than cotton. Plus, reviewers say it feels luxuriously soft and boasts a good weight (basically, it's not see-through). The extra button at the chest prevents gaping, while the hidden button on the wrist makes cuffing the sleeve easier on hot days when you want to roll them up. It offers a streamlined look thanks to its tailored fit and no-pocket design. Sure, it's a little pricey, but considering how easy it is to care for and how long it will last, it's worth the investment in our opinion.

    Size: 00–18 | Material: Polyester | Care: Machine wash, hang dry

    Best Value

    Uniqlo Rayon Long-Sleeve Blouse

    Uniqlo

    What We Love: Wrinkle-resistant and durable, this blouse gives you a lot of bang for your buck.

    What We Don't Love: The collar flops over instead of having a crisp shape.

    As this roundup’s co-author Emily Cieslak prepared for her first job just after graduating college, she stocked up on a few of these blouses from Uniqlo after trying the short-sleeved version during a previous internship. She wanted a versatile, professional-looking shirt she could easily care for without breaking the bank. This blouse fits the bill to a T. It has a Goldilocks fit: not too tight or too oversized. The slim fit worked best for my petite frame because she could tuck it into bottoms without fabric bunching up around my waist.

    Even after being tucked in for eight hours at the office, the shirt never wrinkled. The lightweight fabric feels comfortable during sweltering summer days. Best of all, it's durable — the shirt looks just as good three years later as it did the day she bought it.

    Size: XXS–XXL | Material: Rayon, polyester | Care: Machine wash

Lightweight cotton poplin is breathable and soft, but it’s also slightly sheer, so layer accordingly. You can let your bra peek through or add a tank underneath. We love the crisp look of this fitted button-down, complete with sharp collars and fitted arms. Reviewers note that the shirt is great for formal occasions and work uniforms alike. Despite its formal look, it's also worth noting that this shirt is machine washable, the durable fabric can also hold up to ironing to ensure a crisp fit.

    What We Don't Love: Reviewers say the fabric can be a bit see-through.

High slits on the sides make it easier to tuck the shirt into high-waisted bottoms, which can be extra challenging when dealing with a baggy fit, especially when in the petite size. But to ensure the shirt fits well on everyone, the top comes in both petite and tall sizes to help you find your perfect fit.

    Size: XXS–XXL, tall and petite available | Material: Cotton | Care: Machine wash

Instead of buttons, you’ll find tuxedo-style studs in the metal of your choosing. You can further customize the shirt by choosing the fabric (cotton poplin, voile, or linen) and adding a personalized monogram for an extra $25.

    What to Keep in Mind

    Material

    For a white button down, fabric is everything. For work or fancy occasions, you’ll want to choose crisp cotton like Ayr’s Deep End oxford shirt, our favorite contender. Though it’s less structured, silk can be another great choice for work, and we love Numi's Simone top specifically because it’s also stain resistant (ideal for when you’re eating a sandwich at your desk. For Evans, choosing the right material means thinking about how the shirt will photograph on a celebrity client. “It's always about how it looks in a picture. It's key to the choice of shirt.” Lightweight linen is great for summer styling, while polyester can offer a bit more stretch.

    Size

    The size and style you choose to wear will be guided by your taste and what is comfortable. “If you're on the slimmer end with a rectangle body shape, you want to wear a more relaxed cut or boyfriend cut button-down, which adds more volume to the body and will give you more of a shape,” says Osemwenkhae. In this case, Everlane’s cropped top is a favorite contender. “For an hourglass, a more tailored fitted button-down will be ideal, accentuating the smallest part of your body, and that's your waist.” Benino’s fitted top will comfortably skim your body.

    Transparency

    The worst case scenario would be showing up to a job interview or first date, unaware that your shirt of choice is actually see-through. Look for shirts with heavier fabrics like the button-down from Djerf Avenue to ensure you’re covered. On the other hand, showing off a bra in a contrasting color, like black can be a fun styling choice. So, if you want to show off a little skin, try a light poplin or linen like that used in Aritzia’s boyfriend shirt.

    Your Questions, Answered

    How do I style a white button down?

    Think of a white button-down as the anchor to an endless number of outfits. It’s neutral, it can be worn open or closed, cropped or oversized. “I love wearing a white button-down with a crop sweater and trousers or layered with a bustier and boyfriend jeans and pumps,” says Osemwenkhae. You can dress up sweats like Sarah Jessica Parker, or you can use it to anchor a red-carpet-worthy outfit like Charlize Theron. It runs the gamut from everyday to occasion.

    How do I clean a white button down?

    When your white button-down is in heavy rotation, odds are a sleeve will dip into your plate at some point. Stain-resistant styles like M.M. LaFleur’s are ideal for this exact reason, but there are fixes if you choose a riskier material too. 

    For smaller spots, try soaking or scrubbing out a spot with a mixture of vinegar and castile soap or a stain remover like Shout. Always soak or wash with cold water and allow the garment to air dry. If the stain is difficult, soak the shirt overnight in a solution containing 1/4 cup hydrogen peroxide, vinegar, and baking soda. Lastly, if you must, you can resort to using bleach to banish especially tough stains.

    How far do I button up?

    The nice thing about a button-down is its versatility, there is no right or wrong way to wear it. You can try leaving just a button or two un-done like Taylor Swift, a slightly more undone look to show off your necklaces à la Jennifer Lopez, or like Zoe Kravitz go for showing off your entire bra. While that look may not be right for the office, why not try it for a night out on the town.
