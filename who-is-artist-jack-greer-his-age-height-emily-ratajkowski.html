    Jack Greer Career

    Jack Greer has built his career as an artist known for his multimedia works that range from street photography and documentary video to textiles and engraved live cactus sculptures. However, Jack was labeled as a “jack of all trades, but master of none” because he had worked in fashion as a freelancer to pay the bills until his artworks were sold.

    “Rather than bring that up as a relevant point for discussing my sewing skills, it was something that was pushed behind because all different people — from collectors to curators, to even people working alongside me,” the artist express his concern. But now, he doesn’t care what others say. “If I’m validated, cool. If I’m not, cool,” he added.

    What sets Jack apart from other artists is his eyes to see beauty in daily activities — especially those that are common for people in my life that are outside of the spectrum of what is normal for most people. His arts are people in their teens or early 20s who have open-ended schedules, with the excitement and openness to participate and do things. “They’re not guarded. There is something that is symbiotic about the whole situation that makes it fun for both of us,” he explained.

    Furthermore, Jack takes inspiration from none because he isn’t “familiar with the personalities and the people” in the art world. That’s something new!

    The last project he was working on was a short film called Circles In Tomkins Square — an observational piece shot on tape in the Lower Manhattan public park. He said that it was a visual love letter to the Lower East Side hangout.

    Also, Jack has collaborated with Nike, Opening Ceremony, DSM, White cube London, and Eightball.

    As for his shows, he’s participated in group exhibitions at Museum Dhondt-Dhaenens, Belgium, Th Arts Club, London, Bugada & Cargnel, Paris, Fondazione Museo Pino Pascali, are more. And also, he’s done a solo show with Galerie Rodolphe Janssen in Brussels.

    His first verified exhibition was the “Draw” Tour at Fuse Gallery in New York City, NY in 2006.

    Trivia: Back in 2012, Jack was added to Poster Child Prints’ emerging artists’ roster.
