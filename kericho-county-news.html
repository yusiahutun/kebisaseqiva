    Kericho County is one of the 47 counties in Kenya, located in the Rift Valley region.

    Location

    Kericho County is situated in the southwestern part of the Rift Valley region, bordering Bomet County to the east, Narok County to the west, Nakuru County to the northwest, and Kisumu County to the south.

    County Headquarters

    The county headquarters of Kericho County is located in Kericho town, which serves as the administrative center and the largest urban center in the county.

    Geography and Climate

    Kericho County is known for its picturesque landscapes, rolling hills, and lush green tea plantations.

    The county enjoys a moderate climate, with temperatures averaging between 10°C and 28°C throughout the year.

    Its elevation, ranging from 1,600 meters to 3,000 meters above sea level, contributes to the cool and favorable climatic conditions

    Economy

    Agriculture is the mainstay of Kericho County's economy, with tea farming being the dominant agricultural activity.

    The county is renowned for its vast tea plantations, producing high-quality tea that is exported worldwide.

    Other agricultural activities in the county include coffee farming, dairy farming, horticulture, and subsistence crop farming.

    Tourism

    Kericho County offers several tourist attractions for visitors to explore.

    The tea estates, such as the renowned Ketepa Tea Factory and other tea processing factories, offer guided tours where visitors can learn about the tea-making process and enjoy scenic views of the tea plantations.

    The county is also home to natural wonders like waterfalls, forests, and beautiful landscapes that provide opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and nature walks.

    Culture and People

    Kericho County is predominantly inhabited by the Kalenjin community, particularly the Kipsigis sub-tribe.

    The people of Kericho are known for their warm hospitality, vibrant traditional ceremonies, and cultural practices.

    Traditional dances, such as the Kipsigis dance, are an integral part of their cultural heritage.

    Infrastructure and Services

    Kericho County has developed infrastructure to support its growing population and economic activities.

    The county has well-maintained road networks, including major highways like the Nairobi-Kisumu Highway and the Kericho-Kisii Highway.

    The county government has established schools, healthcare facilities, and other essential services to cater to the needs of the residents.

    General Information

    Kericho County, nestled in the scenic landscapes of the Rift Valley region, is famous for its tea plantations, cultural heritage, and welcoming communities. The county's lush greenery, favorable climate, and agricultural activities make it a delightful destination for both locals and tourists.

    Explore the tea estates, savor a cup of freshly brewed tea, and immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions. Kericho County's natural beauty, warm hospitality, and commitment to agriculture and tourism provide a captivating experience for visitors and a flourishing community for its residents.
