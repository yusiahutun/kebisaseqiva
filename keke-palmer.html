    Keke Palmer is a well-known award-winning American actress, TV personality, and singer. Keke Palmer is best recognized for playing leading and character roles in comedy-drama productions.

    Early Life and Childhood

    Keke Palmer was born Lauren Keyana Palmer on 26th August 1993. She stepped into this world in Harvey, Illinois, United States. By this, she is currently 28 years old and American by nationality. Similarly, she follows the Christian religion and her sun sign is Virgo.

    Talking about his family members, she was born to her father Larry Palmer, and her mother Sharon Palmer. Her father and mother were professional actors. Larry is presently employed at a polyurethane firm, while her mother works as a high school teacher with autistic students.

    Lawrence Palmer, Lawrencia Palmer, and Loreal Palmer are her three siblings, respectively. Keke has always been a gifted young lady. She also did several solo concerts when she was just six years old. She requested her family to move to Los Angeles (LA) when she was nine years old so she could pursue more acting chances.

    Keke Palmer- Professional Life and Career

    Even though Keke Palmer has loved singing since she was a youngster, she made her acting debut in 2004 in the film “Barbershop 2: Back in Business.” She portrayed the niece of Queen Latifah. She landed a job in an episode of CBS’s ‘Cold Case six weeks after moving to LA (2004). She was cast in a national K-Mart commercial and a national search program to act opposite William H. Macy in the film “The Wool Cap” within a year.

    During this time, she auditioned for the ‘American Idol’ spin-off series ‘American Juniors,’ which was subsequently canceled, in an attempt to establish herself as a singer. She signed with Atlantic Records in 2005. Also, she starred in the television movie ‘Knights of the South Bronx’ in 2005. She also had guest appearances on three TV shows in the same year: “Second Time Around,” “ER,” and “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit.”

    In 2006, she was cast in the award-winning film ‘Akeelah and the Bee as the lead part of ‘Akeelah Anderson.’ She had a supporting part in the film ‘Madea’s Family Reunion’ the same year. Her song ‘Tonight’ was included as the opening song in the end credits of the film ‘Night at the Museum’ the following year.

    More Details

    She appeared in the Disney channel’s ‘Jump In!’ in 2007, which also featured her two songs ‘It’s My Turn Now’ and ‘Jumpin’. She also appeared in the thriller ‘Cleaner,’ opposite Samuel Jackson and Eva Mendes. ‘So Uncool,’ her debut album, was released on September 18, 2007, and featured the single ‘Footworkin’. Despite favorable reviews, the album did not fare well on the Billboard charts.
