    Chronixx is a famous reggae artist from Jamaica. He is recognised globally as the face behind the revival of the reggae genre thanks to his vocals, rhythm and lyrics. Many of his songs revolve around themes such as anti-war, resilience, and romance.

    Chronixx has been in the music industry for quite a while. He has impacted many people through his music and life journey. The singer is known for hits such as Here Comes Trouble, Smile Jamaica, Access Granted, Most I, and Mi Alright.

    Chronixx biography

    The singer was born on 10th October 1992 as Jamar Rolando McNaughton. Where is Chronixx from? He is from Spanish Town, Jamaica. He is 28 years old as of 2021.

    He is the son of Selvin McNaughton (father), but there are no details about his mother. His father was commonly known by his stage name, Chronicle. He was a renowned reggae artist and the singer's biggest source of inspiration to join the reggae music sector.

    He joined St Catherine High School in the Spanish town. He has not offered any information about his college studies or the level of education attained.

    Career

    Chronixx started singing at a tender age. He wrote his first song, known as Rice Grain, while he was only 5 years old. He revealed this during one of his interviews with Okay Player as he spoke about his early life.

    My music came from early beginnings, from childhood days. I used to sing at school, in church, and then my whole family sings. My daddy, Chronicle, caused me to be very exposed to music from a very tender age. That’s where the music started for me. Professionally now, that’s when I was in high school. I started producing. Making riddims. But the music goes from then until now. When I was 15, 16, I started producing, and it was a great vibe for me.

    The singer recorded a gospel track with a producer known as Danny Browne at the age of 11. He also provided harmony vocals for artists like Lutah Fyah. He also composed rhythms for various artists such as Munga Hornourebel and Konshens Popcaan under the Mavrick Record label.

    He started producing riddim songs for several record labels at the age of 14. The singer first produced Freezer for Ice Box Records. After his brother died in 2009, he wrote a track with Romaine Arnett of Zincfence Records.
