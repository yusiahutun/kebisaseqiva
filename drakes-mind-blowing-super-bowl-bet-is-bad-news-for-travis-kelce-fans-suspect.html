    25 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Drake has shown his support for the Kansas City Chiefs and the Swifties as he places a huge bet on Travis Kelce and the boys to take home the all-important Super Bowl tonight.

    The music star is known for placing huge bets on sporting games, so of course, the Super Bowl wasn’t going to be any different. Here’s how much Drake‘s betting on Travis Kelce and the Chiefs to beat the San Francisco 49ers, as he can’t ‘bet against the Swifties.’

    Drake ‘can’t bet against the Swifties’ with huge Super Bowl bet

    The God’s Plan singer placed a whopping $1.15 million in Bitcoin on the Cheifs winning the Super Bowl in Vegas tonight, with an estimated payout of $2.34 million.

    The 37-year-old took to his 146 million Instagram to share the screenshot of his bet, alongside the caption “I can’t bet against the Swifties.”

    However, the bet may not be all good news for Taylor Swift and the gang, as some are commenting saying he’s put “the Drake curse” on the Kansas City Chiefs.

    Although the Grammy winner never gets it wrong with his music, he’s been known to ‘get it wrong’ in sports, as the person, or team, he supports or bets on ends up losing.

    “9ers winning. Thanks, Drake,” commented one fan who believes in the curse.

    Another wrote: “Drake just cursed the Chiefs.”

    We guess we’ll have to wait and see what goes down in tonight’s big game!

    It’s not the first time Drake has shown ‘he’s a Swiftie’

    Drake and Taylor Swift fans will remember his iconic Apple Music commercial when the singer secretly listened to Bad Blood.

    The commercial showed Drake working out with his trainer, whilst listening to his own song with 21 Savage, Sneakin’.

    However, as soon as his PT left the gym, the singer hilariously released his inner Swiftie, playing Tay’s hit song, Bad Blood.

    He then proceeded to dance around, work out, and even sing along to the tune, but unfortunately, got distracted and fell off the workout bench.

    Honestly, Drake, we’ve all been there.
